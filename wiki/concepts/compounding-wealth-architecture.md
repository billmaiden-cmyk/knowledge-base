Compounding wealth architecture refers to the three-engine, sequenced asset model at the center of [[Bill Maiden]]'s generational wealth plan. The core principle: stability buys patience, patience enables compounding, compounding creates the base for step-changes, and step-changes create generational wealth. No single asset is asked to do everything; each has a distinct job that creates conditions for the others to compound harder.

## The Three Core Engines

### Engine 1: More Than Strata (MTS) — Primary Owned Equity
**Job:** 100%-owned equity asset that compounds through AI-enabled operating leverage, organic growth, and 2 debt-funded acquisitions. GM-led from Year 2. Bill as architect, not operator.

**Output:** $20.4m equity value at Year 5 + $3.1m after-tax cash distributions over 5 years flowing into Engine 3.

See [[More Than Strata (MTS)]], [[AI-Enabled Strata Operations]], [[Strata Acquisition Arbitrage]].

### Engine 2: Liberty Corporate Finance — Highest Return on Bill's Time
**Job:** Fee income, profit share, dividends, and equity appreciation. Bill's strategic relationships and PE credibility convert directly into deal flow. The cleanest scaling economics in the portfolio.

**Output:** $15.1m equity value at Year 5 + $3.4m cumulative after-tax cash reinvestable over 5 years flowing into Engine 3.

See [[Liberty Corporate Finance]].

### Engine 3: Investment Portfolio — The Compounding Engine
**Job:** Every dollar of after-tax surplus cash from MTS and Liberty flows here. Compounds at 20% gross / 18% after estimated tax drag. Never blows up.

**Starting balance:** $2.0m  
**Net return target:** 18% (after ~2% estimated tax drag on CGT and dividends)  
**Output:** $14.2m at Year 5 | $32.5m at Year 10

## Supporting Assets

**ABGF (4 days/month):** Combined with Liberty salary, covers Bill's full personal overhead so MTS and portfolio compound without being drawn down. Provides institutional credibility.

**VN/AI capability:** Enables MTS operating model. Not a standalone wealth engine but a leverage layer that makes Engine 1 possible.

## Portfolio Discipline

The 18% net return requires active discipline — not just selection skill:
- Maximum single position: 8–10% at cost
- Maximum correlated cluster: 25%
- Barbell structure: 60% high-conviction, 40% stable base
- Hard drawdown limit: 15% from peak triggers mandatory de-risking
- Decision journal: every trade logged with thesis before execution
- Research infrastructure with Antonella: database, Karpathy auto-research, systematic screening

Bill has demonstrated stock selection edge with multiple 5x outcomes. The stated issue has not been selection skill — it has been poor exits, excessive high-beta correlation, and weak portfolio construction.

## Downside Floor

If AI margins fail (MTS stays at $2.5m), Liberty achieves only 50% of deal targets, and portfolio returns 12%: Year 5 net worth is approximately $18–22m. This is described as "still a strong wealth outcome — just not generational on a single-generation timeline."
