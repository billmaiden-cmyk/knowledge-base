AI-enabled strata operations refers to [[Bill Maiden]]'s strategy of deploying AI agent systems and offshore technology resources to transform the cost structure and service quality of [[More Than Strata (MTS)]], enabling the economics that underpin his generational wealth plan.

> **Document timeline:** This concept emerged ~2024 as a strategic thesis (text 2.txt, wealth plan) and has progressively hardened into detailed technical specifications (~2025: world model v.03, R&M agent spec, updated architecture). The step-function cost model was articulated early; the specific agent workflows and substrate design came later as implementation detail. The MTS/SE merger proposal (~2025) represents the most recent thinking on scaling the model.

## The Core Thesis

Traditional strata management businesses are labor-intensive service businesses with EBITDA margins of 15–20%. The MTS plan projects 53% EBITDA margins at Year 5. This margin expansion is almost entirely dependent on AI-driven cost compression enabling each incremental capacity step (2,200 lots) to be served at ~$300k in direct labor rather than conventional linear staffing.

The critical proof point: within 6 months, AI must demonstrate convergence toward 70% gross profit margins on the existing lot base. If this fails, the entire acquisition arbitrage and EBITDA leverage thesis collapses, and the correct decision is to sell MTS and redirect capital.

**Investment and Research Tools:**

## Step-Function Cost Model

MTS uses a step-function rather than linear cost model:

- Each capacity band serves 2,200 lots before a new resource step is needed
- Each new step costs ~$300k/year in direct labor
- Within each band, organic lot additions add revenue at near-zero incremental cost
- At full band utilization, gross profit converges toward the 70% standard
- The model is under-utilized at the start of each new band (lower initial GP), converging as lots fill the band

This model is the foundation of the [[Strata Acquisition Arbitrage]] — acquired books are plugged into spare AI-enabled capacity, not a new cost structure.

## Vietnam (VN) Team Role

The Vietnam team serves two functions:
1. **AI engineering:** Building and maintaining the agent swarm
2. **Operations support:** VN operations staff in the cost model (part of the $160k new direct hires at Day 1) providing labor-cost-efficient human-in-the-loop coverage

The VN team is managed through sprint cycles, not traditional employment structures.

## Risk

The AI margin proof is the single most critical dependency in the entire wealth plan. If the AI does not deliver:
- 70% GP convergence within each capacity band
- 2,200-lot capacity steps at $300k/step
- Meaningful reduction in cost-to-serve vs traditional staffing

...then the acquisition arbitrage disappears, MTS stays at traditional margins, and the correct strategic response is to sell MTS at $2.5m and redeploy into [[Liberty Corporate Finance]] and the investment portfolio.
