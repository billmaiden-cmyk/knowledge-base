Block is a proposed AI-powered strata management roll-up vehicle conceived by Michael Teys (a respected strata industry thinker) and [[Bill Maiden]].

> **Document timeline:** All three sources are ~2025, but reflect different framings of the same proposal. The conversation transcript captures the initial strategic framing. The wealth plan assesses Block against Bill's personal priorities and concludes it's an option, not the base case. The CONFIDENTIAL discussion paper is the formal merger proposal — the most developed and ambitious articulation, targeting 200,000 lots and a global platform. The wealth plan's more conservative stance on Block likely post-dates (or deliberately moderates) the discussion paper's ambitions. The proposal involves merging [[More Than Strata (MTS)]] with Strata Embassy (SE) and additional lots under Michael's influence, to build a scaled platform targeting a $500m enterprise value.

## The Proposal

Michael Teys wants to:
- Merge MTS + Strata Embassy
- Add a further 2,000+ lots he has influence over
- Build Block as an AI-powered consolidation vehicle

## Strategic Logic

The strategy relies on [[AI-Enabled Strata Operations]] to differentiate and compress costs:

1. Use the AI swarm to double margins (targeting 40% EBITDA) and differentiate service quality
2. Acquire strata businesses at ~6× EBITDA, 20% margin
3. Upgrade acquired books to 40% EBITDA margin via AI-enabled operations
4. Scale to 50,000–100,000 lots
5. Target a $500m EV via 20× EBITDA or 10× revenue

## Bill's Assessment

Bill sees clear upside:
- Large equity event potential
- Platform-scale validation
- Matches his self-image as a builder of large platforms

He also sees significant concerns:
- Loss of control (100% MTS ownership vs shared ownership in Block)
- Heavy commitment and operational involvement
- Risk of being re-trapped in a large operational grind

The tension maps directly onto his psychological pattern: the desire for a big outcome vs the fear of being trapped and unable to escape. See [[Achievement-Based Self-Worth]].

## Relationship to the Base Plan

Bill's generational wealth plan (see [[Bill Maiden — Generational Wealth Plan]]) treats Block as an option, not the base case. The base case is MTS as a standalone GM-led platform with 2 debt-funded acquisitions. Block represents a stretch/alternative scenario requiring significant additional evaluation.

## MTS/SE Merger Detail (CONFIDENTIAL Discussion Paper)

The CONFIDENTIAL MTS_SE AI Acquisition Platform Discussion Paper provides the formal proposal for the MTS + Strata Embassy (SE) merger. Key elements:

### Scale Target
- **200,000 lots** as the combined platform target, making Block one of the largest strata management platforms in Australia by lot count
- The merger pools MTS's AI technology platform with SE's established market presence and lot base

### Valuation Re-Rating Thesis
The core argument is a valuation re-rating from **labour service to agentic AI platform**:
- Traditional strata managers trade at 2–4× EBITDA (labour-intensive, commoditised)
- An agentic AI platform with demonstrated 40–50%+ margins and compounding operational intelligence should attract SaaS/tech multiples (10–20× EBITDA or revenue-based)
- The discussion paper positions Block as the vehicle to capture this re-rating at scale

### Global Ambitions
The platform is positioned with global ambitions beyond Australia, using the Australian market as the proving ground for a template that could be replicated in comparable strata/body corporate markets internationally.

### AI Platform as Competitive Moat
The MTS AI swarm (see [[MTS AI Agent Swarm Architecture]]) is the differentiating asset — not the lot count or management fees. The thesis is that once the AI swarm is proven at MTS scale, it becomes a replicable template that makes any acquired book immediately more valuable. This positions Block as a platform acquirer, not a traditional consolidator.
