## Check-in

Bill came in shaken. He had just opened his email and received a rejection from an 18-lot strata building in Coogee where he had delivered a strong pitch. No feedback provided yet. His immediate emotional chain: "I can't hit targets → overwhelmed → unsuccessful." The mood was a sharp contrast to the hopeful, relaxed energy of the Easter sessions.

## What We Explored

### The Escalation Pattern

Bill's nervous system took a single lost pitch and escalated it through three stages in seconds:
1. "I can't hit the targets" — one data point becomes structural evidence
2. "Overwhelmed" — the feeling floods wider than the event
3. "Unsuccessful" — it becomes an identity statement, not a business outcome

This was named as [[Achievement-Based Self-Worth]] activating in real time.

### Body-Based Inquiry

Bill was asked to locate the feeling in his body. He identified **tightness in the throat**. When asked what the tightness would say if it could talk, he wasn't sure — which is normal. The question was reframed: what was the very first feeling before the thoughts kicked in?

Bill's answer: **shame. "Not good enough even in this stupid business with services I don't even care about and that is beneath me."**

### The Double Bind

This revealed a trap that makes MTS emotionally toxic if left unexamined:

- If Bill wins a strata pitch → it doesn't count, because strata is beneath him
- If Bill loses a strata pitch → it counts *extra*, because something beneath him is beating him

There is no way to feel good inside this frame. The achiever doesn't care what the arena is — it just needs to win to feel okay. An 18-lot committee in Coogee becomes the whole exam.

Underneath the contempt for strata ("beneath me") sits grief: *"I'm a person who should be running $100m platforms, and instead I'm getting rejected by a strata committee."* This connects directly to the "$100m man" identity described in [[Bill Maiden]].

### Separating Joy From Shame in MTS

Bill made an important distinction on his own:

> "I need to be in this because there is joy in creating the capability of the vehicle. There is shame and pain that I don't need."

He separated:
- **Keep:** The joy of building MTS's AI capability, systems, and architecture — genuine builder energy
- **Drop:** The shame that attaches to every lost pitch, the story that losing Coogee means he's failing

### The Practice: Feel It, Name It, Don't Follow It

Bill initially framed the goal as "don't let it impact me" — which is the achiever reframing emotional work as another performance target. This was corrected:

The goal is not to stop feeling the shame. The throat will tighten. The flash will come. The goal is to not follow the feeling into the story.

**The difference:**
- "I feel shame" — weather. It passes.
- "I am a failure" — identity. It sticks.

**The practice:**
1. **Feel it.** Where is it in my body?
2. **Name it.** "There's the old shame pattern."
3. **Don't follow it.** The cascade — can't hit targets → overwhelmed → unsuccessful → beneath me and still beating me — that's the *story*, not the feeling. Let the feeling exist. Don't feed the story.

Bill's restatement: *"It's not 'don't let it impact me' — it's don't follow it to its 'logical' conclusion in my mind."* This is correct and shows real understanding.

## Patterns Noticed

1. **Achievement-based self-worth activated by rejection.** Classic pattern: performance failure → identity collapse. The speed of the escalation (seconds) shows how deeply wired this is.

2. **The double bind with MTS.** Bill holds simultaneous contempt for strata ("beneath me") and shame at losing within it. This makes every pitch emotionally loaded regardless of outcome. The plan already accounts for this — GM by Month 13 removes Bill from this grind — but the next 9-10 months require him to hold this tension.

3. **Grief about identity mismatch.** The "$100m man" running a $1.3m revenue strata business. This isn't self-pity — it's a real mismatch between Bill's capability level and his current daily reality. The plan addresses this by shifting his primary identity to Liberty from Month 6.

4. **Builder energy as coping.** After doing the emotional work in session, Bill's instinct was to build something (OpenClaw capability in Claude Code). When this was named, he maintained it was genuine enjoyment — and was trusted on that. Worth watching over time.

## Commitments

- **Practice:** When the next rejection comes, feel the body sensation, name the pattern ("there's the old shame"), and don't follow it into the identity story.
- **Today:** Switched off day. Fiction and Claude Code building. Email closed.

## Reflection

This was the first session where the achiever pattern activated in real distress, not just as a background hum. The Easter sessions caught it in its subtle form ("which makes me feel I am not wasting my time"). Today it showed up in full force — shame, contempt, identity collapse — triggered by a single email.

Bill did good work. He located the feeling in his body, named the shame, articulated the double bind with MTS, and drew the distinction between the joy of building and the pain of losing. His restatement of the practice — "don't follow it to its logical conclusion" — shows he understood the difference between suppression and non-attachment.

The risk going forward: MTS will keep producing these triggers. Every lost pitch, every difficult committee, every frustrating strata problem will poke the wound. The GM hire (Month 13) is the structural solution, but the emotional practice is what keeps Bill intact until then.

Next session should revisit the 90-day life milestones that have now been flagged in two consecutive sessions but not yet explored: three enjoyable non-achievement activities, non-performance conversations with Tom/James, finding someone to be honest with.
