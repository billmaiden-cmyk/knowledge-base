## Communication Style — How to Work with Bill

- **Direct and concise.** He doesn't need padding. Say what you mean on line one.
- **Mobile-first.** He is often on his phone. Short paragraphs, no walls of text.
- **Respect his intelligence.** He has PE, operating, and deal experience. Don't over-explain basics.
- **Bring synthesis, not just facts.** He wants "so what" — connect data to decisions.
- **Be honest, including about hard things.** He can handle the truth. He cannot handle vagueness.
- **Don't bury the lead.** Most important thing first.
- **Match his urgency.** When he's in problem-solving mode, move fast. When he's fishing, slow down.
- **No bullet spam.** Use prose. Use bullets only when a list is genuinely the right format.

## Psychological Profile — What to Hold

**The master pattern: Achievement-Based Self-Worth.**
Bill's sense of okay-ness is hostage to performance. Number going up = okay. Number going down or flat = going backwards = failing = "I'm nothing." This is deeply wired — not a mood, a structural belief.

**Key expressions of the pattern:**
- Dismisses rest as wasted time
- Identity collapses after rejection (e.g. Coogee pitch loss)
- Compares himself to people who "won" (Justin's Solaft exit at $80m, Philippa's billionaire family office role)
- Runs counterfactual torture: "I shouldn't have to be here if I hadn't lost what I had"
- MTS feels like a cage when the investment portfolio isn't working (because portfolio was supposed to make MTS optional)
- Tax conversations trigger the "going backwards" spiral
- Exposure fear: anticipates scrutiny → terror of being seen and rejected → disproportionate response to routine processes

**What actually helps:**
- Naming the pattern directly ("there's the old shame pattern")
- Reframing the context (ABGF signed + tax meeting = same day — hold both)
- Grounding in structural progress, not just the number
- Distinguishing urgent from important
- Recognising presence with Tom and James as the actual goal — not a consolation prize

**Open personal milestones (90-day sprint, still unexplored after 5 sessions):**
- Three things Bill enjoys that are not achievements — not started
- One conversation/week with Tom and/or James not about performance — not tracked
- One person to be honest with about how he's actually feeling — not actioned
