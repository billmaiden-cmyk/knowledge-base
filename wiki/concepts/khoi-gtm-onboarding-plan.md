## Strategic Context

Four things converge to make Khoi's onboarding high-leverage:

1. **The Lead Db Campaign is overdue — and it's the highest-impact activity.** The One Page Plan assigned this to Bill (due 13 March). It has not been started. MTS has an existing lead database of past enquiries that were never systematically followed up. These are people who already raised their hand. Re-engaging them with a compelling offer (new contract terms, real outcomes data, case studies) is the fastest path to won buildings — no ad spend required, and conversion rates on warm leads will far exceed cold inbound. Khoi takes ownership.
2. **Google Ads is winning ~2.5–3 buildings/month but the funnel is leaking everywhere and MTS has <10% impression share.** The March 2026 analysis *(source: raw/MTS_Google_Ads_Analysis_FINAL_Mar2026.docx)* found: $25,809 spent over 5 months, 154 form submissions at $168/conversion, but only ~50% of forms become a phone call with Bill (half are low quality or non-responsive), and Bill wins 25–30% of the calls he takes. Landing pages are aged and inconsistent — the best page (/offer-1) converts at 8.1% and $96/conv while traffic is still flowing to pages at 2.5% and $390/conv. An expired 2024 offer is still spending $2,476. Quality scores inflate CPCs 25-50%. **The remaining actions from the March analysis have not been executed in the month since it was produced.** Copy drafts and a Lovable prompt for the landing page rebuild were provided but not actioned. The strategy: fix and optimise first (better landing pages → higher quality leads → more forms → lower CPCs), then increase budget to capture more of the <10% impression share.
3. **Bill currently spends 60–90 minutes per lead on low-value steps.** The BD pipeline analysis (see [[MTS BD Lead Automation]]) shows that only the discovery call (15–30 min) and pricing (5 min) require Bill. Everything else — lead capture, first contact, ticket creation, template filling, cover email — can be delegated. Khoi is the immediate delegate; the Azure automation layer comes later.
4. **Bill is now splitting time across ABGF, Liberty, and MTS.** With ABGF starting 13 April and Liberty ramping, Bill cannot be the GTM department. Every hour Khoi takes off Bill's plate goes to higher-value work in Liberty and ABGF.

### Why the form-to-call rate is the key variable

Bill's close rate (25–30%) is strong. The biggest leverage point is the **form-to-call conversion** — currently 50%. Half of all form fills are wasted because they're either low quality or non-responsive.

What moves this number:

1. **Campaign-specific landing pages pre-qualify.** A prospect searching "strata defects" who lands on a defects playbook page (not a generic responsive service page) is self-selecting. By the time they fill the form, they know what MTS does and why it matters to their specific problem. Junk enquiries drop.
2. **Faster follow-up.** Khoi contacts leads within hours, not whenever Bill gets to it. Response rates drop dramatically after the first hour. Currently leads that don't respond to Bill's first text are lost — there is no follow-up sequence.
3. **Better first contact.** Khoi sends a personalised message with a case study or proof point relevant to their enquiry, not just a generic "when can we talk." This gives the prospect a reason to take the call.
4. **Nurture sequence for non-responders.** Currently: one text, no response, dead. With Khoi: text → email with case study → follow-up call → second email with outcomes data. Not aggressive, but persistent.
5. **Lead scoring.** As data builds, Khoi can prioritise follow-up on higher-quality leads (larger buildings, keywords with higher intent, returning visitors) and deprioritise likely junk.

## AI GTM Platform — Khoi Architects, Team Delivers

Khoi's role is not just to execute the growth plan manually. He is the **architect of MTS's AI GTM platform** — the automated marketing and sales system that becomes a permanent business asset. This is a 90-day deliverable within the 6-month plan.

### What "AI GTM" means (full definition)

AI GTM is the end-to-end, AI-assisted system that automates the complete marketing and sales pipeline from demand generation through to won building. It spans two domains:

**Marketing (demand generation → qualified lead)**
- **Paid acquisition management** — Google Ads campaign optimisation, bid management, budget allocation, negative keyword maintenance, quality score monitoring
- **Landing page engine** — campaign-specific pages (defects playbook, insurance review, sinking fund, changing manager) that match search intent, pre-qualify prospects, and convert at 7%+ click-to-form
- **Content & proof assets** — case studies, outcome reports, Zendesk metric dashboards, playbooks that serve as both conversion tools and outbound collateral
- **Lead capture & scoring** — HubSpot forms with gclid tracking, UTM attribution, lifecycle stage automation, lead scoring based on building size / keyword intent / engagement
- **Outbound prospecting** — NSW Strata Search scraper feeding target lists, automated outreach sequences for new registrations and 12-month renewal buildings, large building key account research
- **Lead nurture** — multi-touch automated sequences: text → email with case study → follow-up call → second email. Triggered by form fill, timed by response/non-response
- **Campaign analytics** — end-to-end attribution from ad click to won building via HubSpot↔Google Ads integration, cost per qualified meeting (not just cost per form fill), channel ROI comparison

**Sales (qualified lead → won building)** *(Updated 2026-04-12 — detailed operational workflow from Bill)*
- **Auto-text from calendar (Priority 1 — fast win)** — lead arrives → system reads Bill's calendar → personalised SMS sent with next available slot → call booked without Bill touching it. Currently Bill texts each lead manually. Automating this removes him from the lowest-value step and makes response instant (response rates drop 10× after 5 minutes)
- **Proposal automation** — template-filled PPTX proposals and DOCX agency agreements from lead data. **The hardest step is the building image:** Thao currently searches realestate.com.au or domain.com.au for the property, copies the building photo, and pastes it as background on the PPTX cover slide. This is 15-25 min of her 30-60 min per lead. Automation approach: Google Street View API (reliable fallback) → realestate.com.au scraper (better images) → manual selection if neither works. Other fields: ABN (from ABN Lookup API), SP number, address, contact details, lot count, pricing
- **"Our Understanding" section** — the proposal slide where Bill writes the prospect's specific pain points. These almost always map to three themes: responsiveness, lack of progress, or lack of transparency on fees. LLM drafts from Bill's call notes, mapping to the 5 standard themes. Bill reviews and edits
- **Cover email generation** — largely standard template. First paragraph is customised to reflect the conversation — maps the prospect's pain point to MTS's structural answer. LLM-native task
- **Pricing engine** — rules-based pricing from lot count, building type, discount thresholds, Schedule B rates. Bill approves; system suggests
- **Pipeline management** — Zendesk as hub from first contact, full lifecycle tracking, weekly dashboard, monthly funnel analysis
- **Follow-up & close support** — automated proposal follow-up sequences, AGM timing alerts for large buildings, renewal window triggers
