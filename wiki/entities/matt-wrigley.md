## Weekly 1:1 Structure *(Commencing week of 13 April 2026)*

25-minute standing meeting. Progress-based — every item requires a next action with a date, not just a status report.

### 1. Billing *(5 min — always first)*
- Hours billed this week vs **25-hour target** (current stage)
- Explanation for any gap — non-chargeable time summary
- Plan to close if below target
- *Note: 4-week review (w/c 11 May) — target steps to 27.5 hrs/week (5.5 hrs/day). Industry standard for a small Australian legal practice. Bill to name this trajectory in the shaping conversation Mon 13 Apr.*

### 2. Defects / Major Projects *(7 min)*
- Active project count
- On-track vs off-track for each active matter
- Any stalled — what's blocking, what's the plan and when
- New matters needing Bill's awareness
- Each matter must have a next action + date in Zendesk

### 3. Ticket Management *(5 min — conversational, supported by VN ops triage partner)*

Light-touch discussion, not a scoreboard. Matt leads — he talks through where the queue is at, what's been escalated, what's stuck. *(Updated 2026-04-20 — earlier 8-metric dashboard retired as too detailed; VN ops daily reporting to Bill removed. The support is for Matt's benefit, not for surveillance. Background: [[Matt Wrigley — Ticket Portfolio Analysis (19 April 2026)]] and [[Matt Wrigley — Ticket Hygiene Support Plan (VN Ops, 2026-04-19)]].)*

**Discussion prompts:**

- How's the queue sitting overall? Feeling manageable or heavy?
- Anything VN ops flagged as an escalation this week that's worth surfacing?
- Anything stuck or complex where a decision or input from Bill would help?
- Anything coming up on the horizon (compliance deadlines, committee meetings, legal) that could become a priority issue?

**Principle:** if something's broken, Matt raises it. If it's drifting, VN ops should have flagged it to Matt before the 1:1. Bill's job here is to listen and help unblock, not to audit.

### 4. Complex / Legal Matters *(5 min)*
- SP102232 Federal Court status + next action + date
- Any other matters with legal, regulatory, or escalation risk
- Anything requiring Bill's input or decision

### 5. Commitments from last week *(3 min)*
- What Matt committed to at close of last 1:1
- Were they done — yes or no
