The MTS One Page Strategy Plan (March 2026) is the most current single-document articulation of More Than Strata's strategy. It supersedes earlier strategic framing from the Wealth Plan on growth targets and GTM priorities. It organises the business around three pillars: Better Product, Better Business, and Stronger Growth.

> **Note:** Several initiatives in the Priorities/Timeline table had due dates of 13/3 and 20/3 — these are already past as of April 2026. Status of completion is unknown from this document alone.

## Vision and Mission

**Vision:** Building Australia's most responsive, outcomes-driven and AI-enabled independent strata platform. Win by giving committees better advice, tangible progress and faster execution, while building the most scalable and profitable operating model in the sector. Use that operating advantage to compound growth organically and through selective acquisition of underperforming books. Repurpose internal capability tooling to build a "My Strata Manager" agent/financial/workflow platform for non-client Committees and OCs.

**Mission:** Helping communities achieve their goals by managing buildings like (micro) businesses: better advice, improved transparency and faster progress.

## Why MTS Will Win — Competitive Advantages

- Independent position (no conflicts of interest with suppliers) — already resonating with prospects
- Proven responsive, outcomes-driven service model (first reply, resolution, backlog metrics)
- Specialist execution model (instead of generalists)
- Centralised Zendesk-led visibility, triage and control across all work
- AI and workflow capability embedded into operations, reporting and growth
- More scalable platform increasing lots per operator and lowering cost-to-serve

## Three Strategic Pillars

### Better Product
Improve what clients experience:
- Improve owner access (AI Voice Reception; Inhouse Office HQ routed to VN Ops for one-touch resolution of simple queries)
- Improve Committee visibility, activity, progress and outcomes (Committee/Building Report; Chat with Committee Report; Chat with my OC AI Agent; Chat with my Levy Position Agent)
- 3 Page Managing Agency Agreement (simpler onboarding)

### Better Business
Improve how MTS operates internally:
- Centralise work in Zendesk with triage, visibility and control
- Design roles around specialist teams and top-of-scope work (RASCI framework)
- Build AI-enabled operating platform: substrate, orchestration, management intelligence
- Scale lots without linear headcount growth; increase lots per FTE and gross margin
- Key initiatives: Operating Substrate/World Model; ZD/AI Renovations, Keys/Fobs, Audit processes; ZD/AI R&M Agents (Issue Diagnosis, Quote Scoping, Quote Request, Quote Follow-up, Quote Checker, Quote Comparison, Quote Approval); AI AGM Notice Checker; AT Benefits Realisation; ZD Opportunity Assessment/Roadmap

### Stronger Growth
Build the growth engine:
- Build a repeatable acquisition and onboarding engine
- Improve SEM conversion rates
- Leverage existing lead database to generate fresh opportunities
- Manage pricing to improve lot profit
- Use growth operations and automation to scale BD
- Key initiatives: Recruit VN Growth Ops Specialist; leverage existing lead asset DB; build target list, outbound and follow-up engine; develop proposal, proof asset and case study pack; pipeline reporting and growth visibility; progressive website improvements

## Key Observations and Flags

- The GTM hire called out in this plan is a **Vietnam-based Growth Ops Specialist** — not an Australian senior sales hire. This is a lower-cost, high-volume outbound role, not a strategic BD director.
- The Lead Db Campaign (due 13/3) was Bill's personal action item and is already overdue as of April 2026.
- The "My Strata Manager" platform vision in the vision statement is new — a potential future product for non-client OCs built on internal tooling.
- The plan does not mention debt-funded acquisitions explicitly (contrast with the Wealth Plan); acquisition is referenced only in the vision as "selective acquisition of underperforming books."
