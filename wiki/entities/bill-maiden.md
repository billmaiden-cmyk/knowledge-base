Bill Maiden is an Australian entrepreneur, investor, and CEO with a background in private equity and operating leadership. He is the founder and CEO of [[More Than Strata (MTS)]] and holds a 16% equity stake in [[Liberty Corporate Finance]]. He has two sons, Tom (14) and James (12), and a partner, Agnes. His decision-making is shaped by a long history of pressure, trauma, and rebuilding, and his psychological profile is central to understanding his strategic choices.

## Early Life and Psychological Wiring

Bill grew up with very little emotional safety. His mother was unstable and often cruel; his father left when he was around two and was largely absent — seen perhaps ten times across his entire life. He received no baseline of unconditional acceptance from either parent.

What he did receive was praise for achievement: awards and academic recognition at school, then promotions and status at work. This created a core belief embedded in his nervous system:

> *"If I perform and fix hard problems, I matter. If I stop, I'm nothing."*

This pattern drove significant professional success while making his sense of self-worth contingent on external performance. See [[Achievement-Based Self-Worth]].

## Career History

### ANZ Private Equity
Bill joined ANZ Private Equity and built a strong reputation. When ANZ wound up the PE division in 2009, a new leader (Eddie Law) came in to sell off 52 portfolio companies. While many colleagues resisted, Bill cooperated fully, sold companies, and became one of Eddie's trusted lieutenants. He was promoted to Associate Director.

### Solaft / Albany Filtration Technologies
One portfolio asset, Albany Filtration Technologies (later Solaft), became a defining wound. Bill identified on his own — before anyone else — that an equity restructuring he had led (converting a redeemable preference note to equity) would likely trigger commercial debt forgiveness and a potentially fatal tax liability. He sat with this fear alone before raising it. The external directors took the view it was manageable using available tax losses.

Psychologically this locked in as shame, fear of exposure, and hypervigilance. See [[Solaft]].

Bill and his former boss Justin subsequently bought Solaft from ANZ (~US$200k equity, ~US$12m ANZ debt). A large part of this decision was driven by the tax wound — owning it felt like containing the risk of future blame. He completed the Harvard Business School General Management Program during this period, which gave him a taste of a different life, making the return to Solaft feel like "being dragged back into a cage."

By 2014–2015 Bill believed Solaft may have been trading insolvently. He repeatedly raised this at board level, felt unheard, and resigned as a director in June 2015 — legally prudent but emotionally devastating. The company later recovered enough not to collapse, leaving a confusing mix of "maybe I over-reacted" and "no one listened."

### Liverpool Partners
After Solaft, Bill worked at Liverpool Partners in investment banking. He hated the job but pushed through under rising addiction and emotional turmoil.

### Everlight Radiology
Bill joined Everlight Radiology as COO, investing $500k and positioning himself for the CEO role. The business had a major transaction (~$500m) derailed by COVID in early 2020. He was terminated by the PE owners. His $500k investment became ~$2.5m, but he lost the CEO path and professional identity.

### My Emergency Doctor (MED)
Around four weeks after leaving rehab and being fired from Everlight, Bill was hired as CEO of My Emergency Doctor — an emergency telemedicine platform — by Bob Sheraton. He invested ~$800k into MED (now worth ~$200k). He had a catastrophic relapse in August 2020 and went back to rehab while still CEO. See [[My Emergency Doctor]].

## Addiction and Recovery

- **Late 2015–2016:** Intermittent cocaine use — coping, not full addiction.
- **2017–2019:** Full-blown addiction. Heavy, regular cocaine use. Significant spend on drugs and sex workers (~$800k over several years). Life increasingly split between high-functioning executive and destructive compulsions.
- **2020:** Addiction peaks with rehab entries and a catastrophic August relapse (multi-day bender), leading to loss of custody of his sons.
- **2020 onward:** Recovery. 3× weekly urine drug screens, 12-weekly hair tests, supervised parenting time, and sustained effort to rebuild.

## Core Psychological Themes

See [[Achievement-Based Self-Worth]] for the primary psychological driver.

1. **Validation and comparison:** Craves external validation. Compares himself unfavorably to Philippa (billionaire family office role), Justin (sold Solaft for $80m), and MED (continues without him). Core narrative: *"Things do better after I'm gone. I mess up, others reap the value."*

2. **Chronic lack of safety:** Almost never feels safe even when numbers are good. Hypervigilant about tax, lawsuits, insolvency, and loss. Selling Broken Head registers emotionally as dangerous and shameful despite being financially rational.

3. **Unprocessed grief and trauma:** The 2011 tax issue, insolvent trading fear, losing Kate, the affair, addiction, custody loss, supervised access, wealth swings — most survived by pushing forward, not by stopping and grieving.

4. **Relationship with Agnes:** Ambivalent. The relationship was born in chaos and secrecy but has been one of the few constants since everything fell apart.

5. **Identity as "the $100m man":** Believes his natural level is running large platforms and PE-scale outcomes. Running MTS at $1.3m revenue feels like a comedown.

### Patterns surfaced in coaching session 008 (24 Apr 2026, applied to Matt situation but generalisable)

6. **Anger → action reflex.** When activated by a clear violation (Matt missing the 22.5hr target by 10hrs after agreeing 5 days prior), Bill's nervous system reaches for something to do — call, message, solve. The activation wants a release valve. *The work is to slow the reflex enough that the feeling can inform the decision rather than govern it.* Anger as information ≠ anger as instruction.

7. **Standard-projection.** Bill's intensity of expectation can become its own distortion when a direct report doesn't match his own work standard. *"I would never do that"* — meaning "I showed up straight, prepared, delivered" — is legitimate as an operational expectation but can shade into projection when the other person isn't Bill. Useful to notice in moments when anger feels especially personal.

8. **Tolerance-loop.** Bill has been carrying Matt while being frustrated by carrying him — paying $161k for ~13 hrs/week of billing while resenting the gap. *Cannot keep doing both.* At some point in 2026 Bill picks: stop carrying, or stop being frustrated. The Friday session 008 anger was tolerance catching up. Generalisable beyond Matt — anywhere Bill is paying (financially or energetically) for an arrangement that frustrates him.

## What Bill Says He Wants

Beneath the ambition, Bill describes wanting:
- A stable, safe base (financially and emotionally)
- To be a present father
- Meaningful, high-leverage work that uses his mind without destroying him
- Some upside — he does not want to live small
- Actual joy and ease, not just relief between crises
- Not to be driven purely by fear and shame
