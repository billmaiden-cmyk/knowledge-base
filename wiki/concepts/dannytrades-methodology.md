DannyTrades (Danny Cheng) is a Patreon-based investment analyst and former private banker who publishes daily chart analyses. His methodology centres on a proprietary candle colour system combined with institutional flow tracking to identify high-conviction entry points on mid-to-large cap growth stocks. He describes himself as a genuine long-term investor (5–20 year horizon), not a trader.

## 1. The Candle Colour System (Core Framework)

Danny's most important and counter-intuitive concept: **red candles are bullish, yellow candles are bearish**.

| Colour                 | Signal            | Meaning                                               |
| ---------------------- | ----------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| **Red candle**         | **BULLISH**       | Uptrend in force. Hold or add.                        |
| **Yellow candle**      | **BEARISH**       | Downtrend / pullback. Wait.                           |
| **Triple red candles** | **STRONGEST BUY** | Daily + weekly + monthly all red = maximum conviction |

**"Red candle in force"** = the bullish signal is active until a specific closing price level is violated. Danny always quotes the exact invalidation price: *e.g., "the red candle remains valid unless the stock closes below $35.57."*

**"New red candle"** = a fresh bullish signal has just appeared — the most actionable statement in his emails.

**"New weekly red candle"** = highest-timeframe new signal; treated as most significant. Weekly signals override daily noise.

## 2. The Bottoming Process

Danny uses a structured framework to identify when a stock transitions from downtrend to recovery:

1. **Yellow candles appear** — downtrend established, avoid
2. **Volatility hole forms** — transitional consolidation zone, stock in limbo
3. **Long-awaited red candle appears** — bottoming signal; *"the long-awaited red candle has finally appeared"*
4. **Stock closes above specific level** — bottoming confirmed, uptrend resuming
5. **Red candle expands to weekly** — highest-confidence confirmation

He frequently says: *"For the bottoming process to be considered complete, [stock] needs a consistent daily close above $X."*

## 3. Supporting Indicators

### Momentum Bars (Panel 1)
Horizontal price levels displayed in his chart's top panel. These act as the critical support/resistance thresholds. Closing **above the longest/key momentum bar** = uptrend expanding. Each stock has multiple bars at ascending price levels.

### Volatility Holes
Price gap / consolidation zones with defined upper and lower boundaries.
- Stock inside a volatility hole = in transition (bottoming or topping)
- Close **above upper boundary** = bullish breakout
- A new volatility hole during a downtrend = early bottoming signal
- Danny quotes exact upper/lower levels: e.g., *"upper boundary $5.98, lower boundary $4.25"*

### Red/Blue Ribbon (Moving Average Envelope)
- **Red ribbon** = uptrend environment
- **Descending blue ribbon** = downtrend; closing above its upper boundary = trend reversal signal

### Whale Accumulation %
Danny tracks institutional accumulation as a percentage. High % = smart money buying. Increasing whale % during a pullback = high conviction hold. "Whalish momentum" = whale % remaining elevated or rising.

### 20-Month SMA
Key long-term support. A stock bouncing off the 20-month SMA during a pullback is a high-conviction buy zone.

### Other technicals used
- RSI(9): overbought signal when above 90
- MACD golden cross: confirms bullish momentum
- Fibonacci levels (co-charted by "Dr Cat")
- Gann charts (AMD)
- Larry Williams cycle analysis (Nasdaq-100)

## 6. Danny's Investment Philosophy

- **Long-term investor** — 5 to 20 year horizons. Not suited to swing traders.
- **Patience and conviction** are foundational. He does not chase.
- **Does not chart stocks in downtrends** — only covers bullish setups.
- Former private banker who analysed 1–5 stocks/month at US$25–35k/month salary.
- Co-analyses with "Dr Cat" who provides supplementary video insights.
- Midterm election years (2026) historically show drawdowns then strong second-half recoveries — he's positioned for this and calls it explicitly.
- Uses both technical signals AND brief fundamental context (earnings, share repurchases, sector themes).
- Frequently says: *"Stocks always crash — but they keep recovering."*
