a16z Perennial is a multi-family office established by Andreessen Horowitz (a16z), designed specifically for a16z principals and the founders they back. It bridges the gap between retail wealth managers (service-oriented but investment-unsophisticated) and institutional asset managers (sophisticated but optimised for pre-tax, non-taxable capital). CIO is Michel Del Buono.

## The Problem: Two Broken Models

### 1. Traditional Retail Wealth Management
- Compensated via flat AUM fees — no incentive for complex portfolio construction
- Portfolios skew toward simple public equities/bonds
- Strong on service/logistics, weak on investment sophistication

> "You're rewarded as a wealth manager by how much you grow your book of business. You're never trained to be an investment person per se." — Michel Del Buono

### 2. Institutional Asset Management
- Optimised for non-taxable capital (pensions, endowments, sovereign wealth)
- Focus entirely on pre-tax returns
- Fiduciary duty to pre-tax clients means they *cannot* optimise after-tax returns

## The a16z Perennial Approach

Institutional-quality investing implemented through a **tax-aware lens**, customised for concentrated wealth.

### Why Not a Single-Family Office?
- Top investment talent doesn't want to work for one family — they want performance-based comp and a pure investing environment
- Without a professional investment team, a single-family office defaults to fund-of-funds → dual layer of fees

## Key Investment Principles

### Concentrated Stock / Liquidity Events
- Pre-IPO: estate and trust structuring is critical
- Post-IPO: gradual diversification, not wholesale liquidation
- Monetise volatility of concentrated positions without necessarily exiting

### Portfolio Construction (Tax-Aware)

**Real estate** — central role due to structural tax advantages (depreciation deductions); solid teens return on a tax-adjusted basis

**Capital loss harvesting** — losses are most useful *inside* a fund vehicle where they can offset gains internally; personal-level losses are far less flexible

**Caution on SPVs and private credit** — SPVs often structured with high fees and limited control; private credit is tax-inefficient for individuals

### Volatility as Opportunity
With a deep balance sheet and sufficient liquidity, market volatility is a buying opportunity, not a threat.

> "Volatility is not the enemy... if you have a deep balance sheet and you've kept your portfolio at least somewhat liquid, volatility is a huge opportunity." — Michel Del Buono

## Relevance to Bill's Wealth Architecture

The a16z Perennial model validates several principles in [[Bill Maiden — Generational Wealth Plan]]:

1. **Tax-aware compounding** — Bill's plan already separates pre-tax and after-tax cash flows; Perennial's approach reinforces that after-tax return is the only return that matters for individual wealth
2. **Real assets bias** — aligns with Bill's real estate allocation within the investment portfolio engine
3. **Concentrated position management** — relevant to Bill's 16% [[Liberty Corporate Finance]] stake and 100% [[More Than Strata (MTS)]] ownership; gradual diversification principles apply when liquidity events occur
4. **Multi-family office as aspirational structure** — as Bill's wealth scales past $50M+, the Perennial model represents the kind of institutional wealth management infrastructure to build toward

# Liberty Perennial Wealth Management — The CEO-Side Trust Wedge

## The Real Edge

Not financial engineering. Not PE expertise. Not even AI capability. The edge is:

> **You are trusted by CEOs as the people representing them and only their interests.**

This is rare and valuable because most people around a CEO in a transaction are:

- paid by the company
- paid by the fund
- paid by the buyer
- paid to get the deal done
- or subtly aligned with someone else's economics

If the CEO experiences you as: on their side, commercially sharp, understanding incentives, understanding how wealth really gets created and lost, and not trying to sell them a generic wealth-management product — then you have a very strong wedge.

## What the Business Really Becomes

Not "wealth management." Not "family office." Not "fund platform."

It starts as: **CEO-side capital counsel.**

From there, expand into:

- liquidity planning
- personal balance-sheet design
- family office-lite coordination
- capital allocation framework
- curated access to deals, SPVs, and opportunities
- eventually private investment vehicles

But the trust wedge comes first.

## Why This Is Powerful

CEOs at liquidity events are:

- newly liquid or about to be
- under-advised on personal capital strategy
- vulnerable to being sold to
- often isolated
- often emotionally disoriented after years of building and one big transaction

The people they trust most are not necessarily the biggest names. They are the people who understand the game, have seen the traps, and are **clearly on their side.**

This creates:

- sticky relationships
- repeat engagement
- referrals
- co-investment opportunities
- LP base for future vehicles
- long-duration trust

## The Product Ladder

### 1. CEO-Side Transaction and Post-Liquidity Strategy (Entry Product)

Not "we can manage your capital" — instead: **"We help you think and act like an owner of capital after the deal, while remaining fully on your side."**

- Incentive and deal review
- Proceeds architecture
- Tax / trust / structure coordination
- Concentration risk
- First-12-month plan

### 2. Ongoing Capital Office

- Quarterly review
- Integrated reporting
- Specialist coordination
- Capital allocation discipline
- Family / legacy structuring

### 3. Curated Access

- Selected co-investments
- Special situations
- SPVs
- Eventually a fund platform

The trust relationship is what makes all of that possible.

## Critical Constraint: Protect the Edge

Do not ruin this edge by becoming obviously conflicted too early:

- Don't start by pushing product
- Don't start by pushing a fund
- Don't start by looking like just another wealth salesman

The whole model only works if the client still feels: **"These people are in my corner."**

Productisation must be done very carefully.

## How the Pieces Connect

| Piece | Role |
|---|---|
| [[Liberty Corporate Finance]] | Gives access to the CEOs at the moment of liquidity |
| Bill's lived experience (Solaft, recovery) | Gives authenticity — he's seen how wealth gets created *and* lost |

## The One-Line Truth

> **The edge is not access to deals. The edge is trusted alignment with the CEO before, during, and after liquidity. Deals and capital products come later as a consequence of that trust.**
