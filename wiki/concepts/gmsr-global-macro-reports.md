## Report 1: "In Something Else We Trust" — October 2, 2025

**Focus:** USD trust breakdown and implications for alternative stores of value (gold, Bitcoin, commodities)

### Core Thesis

The US populace has lost trust in government and politicians — Weldon traces this to a long history of deception since WWII, beginning with the Bretton Woods Agreement. This trust breakdown is reflected in USD secular weakness.

### USD Technical Picture (as of October 2025)

- The USD index (USDX) had experienced a 25-year bear market from the Plaza Accord high of 164.72 (1985) to the 2008–2011 double-bottom
- A 2011–2022 bullish correction mapped as an A-B-C Elliott Wave 50% Fibonacci retracement in both time and price
- Key downside pivot: **96.21** — a monthly close below this level would confirm a new secular bear market in the USD
  - Already sitting in the "Depreciation Zone": 1-year and 2-year rate-of-change indicators show USD decline
  - 2-year USD depreciation of approximately (-) 8.5%

### Bitcoin and Gold as Trust Alternatives

- Gold and Bitcoin both rising as "stores of value" amid USD trust breakdown
- Bitcoin had **outperformed NVIDIA** over the prior 52 weeks by +27.5% (as of October 2025)
- Bitcoin was technically positioned for a longer-term upside breakout
- Commodities (DBC Commodity Index ETF) showing longer-term bullish breakout, with confirmation signal at $23.32

### Macro Signal Relevance to Trading

The USD trust/depreciation thesis supports the "Horizon" long-term sleeve's crypto allocation — particularly Shardi B (event-driven/on-chain) and Viv (mean-reversion altcoins) strategies described in [[AI-Driven Trading System (Multi-Strategy Hedge Fund)]].

## Report 2: "Medication Time Gentlemen" — May 28, 2025

**Focus:** US equity market structure analysis (S&P 500)

### Core Finding: Extreme Neutrality

As of late May 2025, the S&P 500 showed extreme internal neutrality:
- ~30% of S&P 500 stocks trending (15% bullish, 15% bearish)
- **70%+ of S&P 500 stocks NOT trending** — one of the highest readings Weldon had seen in "many moons"
- This level of neutral non-trending behaviour was atypical and suggested the market was at an inflection point

### Weldon's Framing

Despite the neutral reading, Weldon noted it "may, or may not surprise you" that he was considering upside breakout scenarios for US stock indexes — with the crucial caveat that leadership was narrow and highly concentrated.
