## Deliverable 2 — Daily Standup Redesign

*Kat's action: redesign the format. Current problem: devolves into complaint sessions with no metrics anchor.*

### Proposed Format (15 minutes, hard stop)

**Minute 0–3: Numbers first**
Kat opens with three figures — no commentary until all three are on the table:
- Yesterday's first reply median (actual vs. 4hr target)
- Current open backlog count (vs. last week)
- Any tickets > 5 business days unresolved

**Minute 3–10: Three questions only**
1. What did we clear yesterday that's worth naming? (wins, however small)
2. What's stuck and needs a different approach today?
3. What needs escalating — and to whom?

**Minute 10–15: Today's priorities**
Each team member states their single most important ticket/task. Nothing else.

### Ground rules
- No war stories, no client rants in the standup itself — those go to a dedicated async channel or a separate 1:1 with Kat
- If it takes more than 30 seconds to explain, it's not a standup item — park it and talk after
- Kat owns the clock. Cut conversations that drift.

### What changes immediately
- Bill will not attend daily standups going forward — Kat runs it, reports up weekly
- Kat flags to Bill: any day the meeting runs over 15 minutes, note the reason in the weekly report

## Deliverable 5 — Expanded Metrics Framework

*Investigation action: metrics beyond first reply times that give a fuller picture of team performance.*

### Why first reply alone is insufficient
The huddle identified that Matt has 48 unreplied messages not captured in standard first reply metrics. This reveals a gap: the current metric measures speed of first reply on tickets that *have* been replied to — it doesn't capture tickets that have been *ignored*.

### Proposed additional metrics

| Metric | What it measures | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Unreplied ticket count (> 24 hrs, no reply ever) | Tickets ignored, not just slow | Catches the Matt problem — evasion vs. speed |
| Ticket allocation distribution | How work is spread across team members | Flags if one person is carrying disproportionate load |
| Work type distribution | Mix of R&M / insurance / admin / complex | Ensures specialist capacity is matched to ticket types |
| Escalation rate per team member | How often Kat has to step in per person | Proxy for team self-sufficiency |
| Offshore utilisation rate | % of triage handled offshore vs. onshore | Tracks progress on capability development goal |
| Tickets created vs. solved ratio | Net backlog trend | Leading indicator of backlog blow-out |

### Recommended implementation
- Unreplied count: already available from Zendesk CSV pull (see Matt Status Report tool — the `no-reply-ever` field). Extend to all team members, not just Matt.
- Ticket allocation: Zendesk assignee breakdown by week — Kat can pull this in her weekly report
- Work type: Zendesk tag/form breakdown — requires consistent tagging discipline (Kat to enforce)
- Escalation rate: Kat to manually log in weekly report for now; automate later via Zendesk if tagging allows
