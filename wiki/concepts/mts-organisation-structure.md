The MTS organisation structure document (July 2025) defines the specialist team model that replaced the traditional generalist strata manager model. It articulates the founding philosophy, structural principles, the org chart, four core role types, and the RASCI framework for accountability. *(source: raw/Organisation Structure_20250701.pdf)*

## Context and Founding Philosophy

Bill founded MTS in 2019 after a poor personal experience as a strata customer — poor responsiveness, poor outcomes, and buildings not managed like businesses. The business was built on seven operating principles:

1. **Manage buildings like they're a business** — OC = company; Strata Committee = Board; MTS = General Manager
2. **Service level is about demand and supply** — match capacity to work volume; if capacity lags, service always degrades
3. **Support top of scope to deliver value** — most experienced people do most complex work
4. **Not generalists; be specialists** — strata manager job is too broad to be great at all of it
5. **Coordinate to have a single view of truth** — Zendesk as the central operational system
6. **Responsiveness: acknowledge and action** — 4-hour first reply target
7. **Outcomes: get a plan, in control, deliver progress** — clients want results, not just acknowledgement

By the time of this document (~July 2025), MTS managed over 100 buildings, having grown from 60 to 105+ in one year. *(source: raw/Organisation Structure_20250701.pdf)*

---

## New Structure Principles

- **Difference between functions and jobs** — at MTS's size, people span multiple functions (e.g., Bill spans Accounting + BD; Matt spans Governance + Fire)
- **Traditional Strata Manager role is broken up** — R&M, Insurance, and Fire are separate specialist functions
- **Top-of-scope focus** — Kat, Matt, and Mary shift to doing only what they uniquely can do; routine work handled by junior/offshore/AI
- **Senior Strata Manager role shifts** toward Governance, Advice, Outcomes, Reporting, and Review — not operational execution
- **Operations Group** — Kat leads a group delivering specialist functions in support of Strata Managers
- **Overwatch function** — Kat maintains portfolio-wide visibility of First Reply Times and Final Resolution Times

## Four Core Role Types

*(source: raw/Organisation Structure_20250701.pdf)*

### Operations Manager (Kat)

**Purpose:** Coordinate Specialist Teams with Senior Strata Managers so clients experience responsive service and achieve desired outcomes in timely ways.

**Key accountabilities:**
- Triage inbound work to the right resource
- Overwatch portfolio service responsiveness (target: 4-hour first reply median)
- Coach team members on responsiveness

**Key outcomes:**
- Specialist Teams fulfil tasks in organised ways with visibility of progress
- Service levels within target ranges
- Effective Zendesk usage across team

---

### Senior Strata Manager (Mary, Matt)

**Purpose:** Ensure Owners Corporations deliver their obligations and achieve their desired outcomes. Hold ownership and accountability for Specialist Team work through review and sign-off.

**Key accountabilities:**
- OCs have clear plans and objectives, achieving them in timely ways
- OCs have visibility of financial position, compliance, and project progress
- OCs fulfil their obligations
- Deliberate, structured Committee engagement
- AGMs and governance tasks performed in timely ways
- Clear tasking of Specialist Teams + follow-up/ownership
- Review and quality sign-off of Specialist Team work

**Note:** Senior Strata Manager is *accountable* for work even if they didn't execute it — e.g., accuracy of levies, meeting agendas, allocation of expense codes.

---

### Specialist Team Lead (Kat for R&M/Insurance, Matt for Fire/Defects)

**Purpose:** Lead the team to deliver the function's requirements. Create repeatable processes. Coach team members. Solve complex issues.

**Key outcomes:**
- Specialist function requirements met to due dates and standards (AFSSs submitted, insurance renewed, levies issued)
- Functions operated efficiently and at lowest cost
- Complex issues managed effectively with clear plans and visible progress
- Strata Manager kept informed of status
- Team members developing within roles

---

### Specialist Team Member (Wins, Thao, Keira, VN team)

**Purpose:** Perform specialist tasks diligently and drive to full completion.

**Key behaviours:**
- Complete tasks per prioritisation and due dates set by Team Lead
- Perform to high quality standard (reduce rework)
- Maintain visibility of work status — report progress
- Get sign-off from Strata Manager/Team Lead where relevant
- Be mindful of bigger picture

**Key outcomes:**
- Tasks completed on time to standards
- OC requirements met through Team Member contributions
- Strata Managers able to deliver outcomes for OCs
- First reply targets met

## RASCI Framework

MTS uses the RASCI model to assign roles and responsibilities across tasks:

| Letter | Role | Description |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **R** | Responsible | Person/people who do the work ("doers") |
| **A** | Accountable | Person ultimately answerable; approves the work (one per task) |
| **S** | Supportive | Provide resources/assistance; help but not directly responsible |
| **C** | Consulted | Subject-matter experts whose input is required before finalisation |
| **I** | Informed | Need to be kept updated; not actively involved |
