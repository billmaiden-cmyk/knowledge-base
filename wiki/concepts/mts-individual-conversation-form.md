MTS's structured annual performance conversation template. Used between managers and team members once per year; the completed form becomes a working reference document for Individual Check-ins throughout the year. The format is employee-led: the team member drives the conversation and completes the form after the meeting. *(source: raw/Individual Conversation Form_MTS.docx)*

---

## Purpose and Philosophy

The form is designed as a two-way reflective conversation, not a top-down performance review. Key design principles:

- **Employee-led** — team member drives the agenda and completes the form post-conversation
- **Not a tick-box exercise** — questions are prompts, not mandatory; not all must be covered
- **Living document** — completed form becomes the basis for check-ins throughout the year
- **Feedback-first** — team member collects peer/stakeholder feedback before the conversation

---

## Structure

### Before the Conversation
- Team member agrees with manager who to collect feedback from
- Team member makes notes on both Looking Back and Looking Forward sections
- Peer/stakeholder feedback shared with manager in advance
- Notes are private unless team member chooses to share

### During the Conversation
- Team member captures input in real time
- Both parties share reflections on the past year
- Career aspirations discussed; strategy alignment agreed
- Priorities for the upcoming year set
- Development Plan co-created
- Team member provides manager with feedback on what support they need

### After the Conversation
- Team member writes up the form from discussion notes
- Shared with manager for comments; both agree final version
- Copy kept by team member; copy sent to manager
- Form referenced at every Individual Check-in throughout the year

---

## Section 1 — Looking Back

*Reflects on the past year's achievements and overall contribution.*

**Team member self-reflection prompts:**
- What am I most proud of?
- What feedback did I receive?
- What did I learn?
- What strengths did I draw on?
- What was my greatest collaborative achievement?
- How did I contribute to the success of others?
- How did I balance getting results with living with the values?
- What business outcomes did I have the greatest impact on?

**Manager reflection prompts:**
- What was their greatest impact on business outcomes?
- What was their greatest contribution to the success of others?
- How did they leverage their strengths?
- How did they balance getting results with living with the values?
- What are the greatest shifts they've achieved in areas they've been working on?
- How have they been doing against expectations for their role?
- What shifts can they make for the greatest future impact?

---

## Section 2 — Looking Forward

*Explores career aspirations and alignment to MTS strategy.*

**Career Aspirations:**
- My aspirations for the next 1–3 years
- What career goals do I have?
- What learning opportunities am I looking for?
- What skills or experience would I like to develop?
- What do I see as my role in delivering on the strategy? What inspires me about it?

**Supporting the delivery of the MTS Strategy:**
- What do I need to do to support delivery of the strategy?
- How do I support others to deliver on the strategy?

**My Priorities** (next few months):
- Free-text area for near-term objectives
- Structured table: Objective | Activities on the job | Support & resources | Target date | Behaviours

**Supporting My Success:**
- What do I need from my Manager to enable me to succeed?

---

## Section 3 — Development Plan

*Key professional development focus areas for the year.*

The 70-20-10 rule is referenced as the learning framework:
- 70% on-the-job experience
- 20% from others (feedback, coaching, mentoring)
- 10% formal learning

Development Plan table: **Objective | Actions | Success Criteria | Timing | Support**
