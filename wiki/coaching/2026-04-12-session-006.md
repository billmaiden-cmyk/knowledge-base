Brief session. Bill messaged from phone — not a formal coaching block but a real activation that needed working through before Monday.

## Check-in

Bill opened angry. Matt Wrigley has billed ~13 hours/week for two consecutive weeks against a 22-hour target and $303.60/hr charge rate. The language was activated ("fucking mad with this prick", "thumbs his fucking nose at me"). The surface content was a management problem; the emotional content was something sharper — a man carrying two new roles (ABGF starting Monday, Liberty as 0.5 FTE Partner) to fund MTS growth, watching a team member not pull his weight.

## What We Explored

### The Source of the Anger

The anger isn't just about billing. Bill named it plainly: "I've taken on two new jobs to pay for MTS growth and he's not pulling his weight." This is legitimate. The ABGF and Liberty commitments are specifically structured to buy MTS the runway it needs to transform. When a senior resource delivers $10k gross profit on a $162k cost base, the deal Bill has made with himself — personal sacrifice buys team performance — is being broken. The anger is an owner's anger, not an irrational reaction.

Key coaching move: validate the commercial reality rather than pathologising the emotion. This is not Achievement-Based Self-Worth acting up. This is a $120k/year performance gap from a backsliding employee on the eve of the most important professional week of Bill's wealth plan.

### The Lift-Then-Backslide Pattern

New information: Matt has a documented pattern of performing well after direct conversations, then gradually falling back. This changes the coaching framing. It's not a first-time diagnostic conversation — it's a pattern that hasn't been caught early enough because there was no weekly accountability structure. The 1:1 design is the structural fix for the pattern, not just a management nicety.

### Break-Even Analysis

Worked through the numbers together:
- Matt's cost: $145k salary + $16,675 super = $161,675/year
- Effective billable weeks: 48 (4 weeks AL)
- Cost per billable week: $3,368
- Ex-GST charge rate: $276/hr
- Break-even: 12.2 hours/week
- At 13 hours: ~$10,500 gross profit/year — effectively breakeven
- At target 25 hours (5 hrs/day, $6,900/week ex-GST): ~$169,000 gross profit/year
- Foregone margin gap: **~$159,000/year**
- *Note: Session used 22 hrs as initial estimate. Recalibrated Sunday evening — correct current target is 25 hrs/week (5 hrs/day = $6,900 ex-GST). In 4 weeks, target steps to 27.5 hrs/week (5.5 hrs/day, industry standard small Australian legal practice). Two public holidays fell in the assessment period — reduces single-fortnight comparison by ~4–5 hrs/week but does not alter the structural gap.*

Bill arrived with intuitive commercial concern. The numbers confirmed and sharpened it — and the recalibrated target makes the gap larger, not smaller.

### Fix or Build the Case?

Named the binary directly. Bill chose fix. This shifted the rest of the session from emotional validation to conversation design.

### Conversation Design — Disappointment as Register

Explored the difference between anger and disappointment as the emotional register for tomorrow's conversation. Anger gives the other person something to push against. Disappointment lands and sits. Agreed that disappointment is the more authentic and more effective frame — Bill has seen Matt perform well; he's disappointed that the pattern keeps repeating. That's harder to dismiss than anger.

Agreed opening: *"Matt, I want to be straight with you. I'm disappointed. I've seen what you're capable of — your first weeks showed me that. And something changes and the billing falls away, and we're back here again."* Then stop. Let silence do the work.

### 1:1 Structure Designed

Full weekly 1:1 agenda developed and written to Matt's wiki page. Five sections: Billing (always first), Defects/Major Projects, Ticket Management, Complex/Legal Matters, Prior Commitments close-loop. AFSS excluded at Bill's direction. Progress-not-status discipline as the design principle — every agenda item requires a next action with a date.

Non-chargeable time tracking introduced as a standing element given Matt is a 100% chargeable resource with a backslide pattern. Acknowledged earlier coaching pushback on this was too quick — at 100% chargeable with a repeating pattern, you need to know where the time goes.

### Monday Sequencing

Conversation with Matt is a shaping conversation — sets up the 1:1 structure, names the billing performance, names the pattern. Not the 1:1 itself. Cadence starts next week. Bill has ABGF at 2:30pm — the Matt conversation needs to be closed before he gets to Carrington Street.

## Patterns Noticed

1. **Legitimate owner anger.** This session is different from previous ABSW activations. Bill is not measuring himself against a phantom benchmark. He's holding a direct report accountable for commercial underperformance. The anger is grounded in real data. Coaching work is to help him channel it rather than dismiss it.

2. **MTS performance as personal affront.** The "thumbs his fucking nose at me" framing suggests Bill experiences Matt's underperformance as a relational act — disrespect rather than just underperformance. Worth watching. If this pattern continues, the conversation about whether the relationship is salvageable will surface.

3. **Structural fix over emotional conversation.** Bill moved quickly from emotional activation to wanting design. That's growth from earlier sessions where the emotion was harder to work with. He named fix vs build-the-case unprompted and chose fix. That's a mature management call under pressure.

## Commitments

- Have shaping conversation with Matt Monday before ABGF, from disappointment register, not anger
- Use the five-part 1:1 structure from next week
- Ask Matt directly: "what's getting in the way of 22 hours" — and genuinely listen before closing
- Do not carry the Matt activation into ABGF at 2:30pm — conversation must be closed and filed

## Coach Reflection

Short but dense session. Bill came in hot and needed to work through the anger before he could get to design. The progression from "fucking mad" to "we need to fix" to a complete 1:1 structure in one conversation is significant — it shows the coaching container is working.

The ABGF context matters. Monday is the most important professional day in Bill's wealth plan. He cannot afford to walk into ABGF carrying unresolved Matt energy. The coaching work this session was partly about protecting that afternoon by giving the morning conversation a complete, closeable shape.

The $120k foregone margin number is the most useful artefact of this session — it gives Bill a commercial anchor that removes the subjectivity from the conversation. He's not just disappointed. He's holding Matt to account for a six-figure margin gap.

Worth exploring in a future session: whether the "thumbs his fucking nose at me" framing is telling us something about how Bill experiences authority — specifically, when he feels disrespected by people he has trusted with senior roles.
