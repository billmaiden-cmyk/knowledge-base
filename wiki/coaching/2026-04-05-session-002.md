Second coaching session, same day as the first. Bill came back with energy and a specific strategic idea to explore.

## Check-in

Bill came in with excitement — not distress, not avoidance. He wanted to think strategically about building a wealth management / investment platform alongside Liberty Corporate Finance. The energy was genuine builder energy, not the achievement-driven restlessness observed in the morning session. He was explicit that he didn't want to execute now — he wanted to shape the idea with the end in mind.

## What We Explored

### Liberty Perennial — The Idea

Bill's core insight: Liberty's strongest asset is not deal flow or PE expertise — it's the trusted relationship with CEOs at the moment of liquidity. These CEOs are newly liquid ($300m+ EV deals), under-advised, and trust Liberty because Liberty was on their side during the transaction.

The conversation evolved through several stages:

1. **Initial framing** — Bill proposed a wealth management business alongside Liberty with PE fund (proprietary deals) and listed equities (Antonella trading program) sleeves
2. **Trust wedge** — Bill had already written a strategic note (added to the wiki) identifying "CEO-side capital counsel" as the entry point, not product-pushing
3. **Wealth management vs capital office** — Bill pushed back on an artificial distinction between the two, correctly identifying that the underlying service is the same — what matters is the order of operations and positioning, not the label
4. **Integrity test** — Bill articulated the fee model clearly: "You pay us for advice in a transaction. You pay us for advice about how to manage your capital. You pay us when you deploy capital with us." Every fee visible. No hidden economics. This passes the integrity test
5. **Principal-advisory framing** — The business is not wealth management and not pure advisory. It's an integrated principal-advisory platform where advisory earns the trust and principal capability earns the returns
6. **Cash flow modelling** — Built a 10-year cash flow model: ~$67m pretax net cash over 10 years across three legs (advisory retainers, PE fund fees/carry, listed equities fees/performance)
7. **Comparable models** — Identified Founders Fund, Raine Group, GP Bullhound, and a16z Perennial as relevant comps. Liberty's structural advantage over Founders Fund: the advisory business is a deal-flow engine for both clients and investment opportunities

### The Antonella Connection

The listed equities leg maps directly to the Antonella trading program already being developed. This gives the program strategic significance beyond Bill's personal portfolio — if it proves out over 12-18 months of live trading, it becomes the engine inside a fund vehicle offered to Liberty Perennial clients.

## Patterns Noticed

1. **Healthy relationship with the idea.** Bill was explicit: "I'm fine not executing it — I just want to get your take." This is the mature version of the builder pattern — shaping without grasping. The achiever was present but not driving
2. **Clear thinking under excitement.** Bill caught the coach overcomplicating the wealth management vs capital office distinction and pushed back directly. Good sign — he's thinking for himself, not deferring to the coach
3. **Integrity as a design constraint.** Unprompted, Bill framed the fee structure through the lens of honesty and alignment. The positioning "we are on your side" isn't marketing — it's a genuine value he wants to build into the business architecture
4. **Strategic integration.** Bill naturally connected the Antonella trading work to this broader platform vision — seeing how the pieces fit together across his portfolio of activities

## Commitments

No execution commitments — by design. This was a "shape it now, execute later" session. The output is a wiki page capturing the full platform concept and 10-year cash flow model.

## Reflection

This was a good session. The energy was clean — excitement about a genuinely strong idea, not restlessness or avoidance. Bill's instinct to shape without executing shows real growth in his relationship with ambition. He's holding the idea lightly rather than white-knuckling into immediate action.

The Liberty Perennial concept is strategically sound and may be the highest-leverage idea in Bill's entire wealth architecture. The trust wedge is real, the economics are real, and the proprietary deal flow is a genuine moat.

The coaching task going forward is to help Bill hold this idea in its proper place — a Year 2-3 initiative that gets shaped by the decisions he makes now, not a shiny object that pulls him away from the 90-day sprint priorities (MTS AI margins, GM hire, Antonella validation, Liberty deal flow).

Next session should check on the 90-day life milestones that haven't been discussed yet: three enjoyable non-achievement activities, non-performance conversations with Tom/James, finding someone to be honest with.
