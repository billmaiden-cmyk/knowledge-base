## Check-in

Bill's first day back at work after the Easter break. He came in listing a full day: fishing with James before a call with Farago, meeting a new tax accountant, and going into the MTS office with Mary. The energy was loaded — multiple sources of dread stacked on top of each other, with the tax meeting carrying the most emotional weight.

He named fishing with James as first on the list but almost dismissed it — "I don't want to go fishing. He loves it." When reflected back, he could see it for what it is: showing up for his son on his son's terms. Love, not obligation. But the anxiety about the day ahead was pulling him forward past it.

## What We Explored

### Tax Fear — "Going Backwards Before Forwards"

The new tax accountant meeting triggered a cascade. Bill is scared of hearing the FY25 liability — hoping it's not more than $50K. But the number itself isn't the real source of distress. The deeper fear:

- Tax problems have been compounding for years (history of $1.6m ATO debt)
- Recent portfolio drawdown has reduced his capital base
- He feels he's going backwards, not compounding toward recovery
- With less to invest, the plan feels harder to execute
- The gap between where he is (~$5m) and where he was (~$9-10m at peak) feels like evidence of failure

When asked directly: **"Is there anything that could make you feel okay that isn't the number going up?"** — Bill said no. Then refined it: having a robust structure for building, a plan he believes he can execute. But acknowledged that plan is "just forming."

### The Core Pattern — Activated Again

This is [[Achievement-Based Self-Worth]] in its purest financial form. Bill's emotional baseline is hostage to the trajectory of his net worth. Number going up = okay. Number going down or flat = going backwards = failing = "I'm nothing."

Key reframe offered: the peak net worth (~$9-10m) existed during active addiction, custody loss, and life in crisis. That wasn't a foundation — it was a high-water mark on a flood. "Going backwards" from that marker is a misleading frame.

### The Gap

Bill is in a structural gap: the old financial structure (crypto gains, Broken Head equity) has been consumed by tax, legal costs, and the MTS investment. The new structure (Liberty, PE role, MTS AI transformation, investment portfolio) is forming but not yet producing. He's hearing costs (tax liability) before he can see the engine that pays for them. That's genuinely uncomfortable — not irrational, but amplified by the pattern.

### The Counterfactual Torture — "I Shouldn't Be Here"

Bill escalated emotionally: "I shouldn't have to execute on this plan had I not lost where I was at, or had the $500K drawdown and instead had a $500K gain — which would put me $1m up on where we are now." The counterfactual ($500K gain instead of $500K loss = $1m swing) is mathematically real but emotionally weaponised. He's measuring today against a scenario that didn't happen and calling himself a failure for the gap.

Named the frame: **"I shouldn't be here"** — not physically, but existentially. At this point in life, at this net worth, having to build again. The hurry he feels isn't strategic urgency — it's the achiever's terror that if he's not ahead, he's disappearing.

Reframe offered: there is no cosmic timeline. The schedule he's behind on was written by the part of him that believes he only matters if he's ahead. And that part is comparing him to a version of himself that was in active addiction.

### MTS as Cage — The Investment Link

The session's sharpest insight came late. Bill said plainly: **"It's the investing not working that's hurting and keeping me grinding at MTS."**

This reframes the MTS avoidance pattern (Sessions 003, 004, 005). It's not just that MTS feels beneath him or triggers shame. It's that the investment portfolio was supposed to be the third engine — compounding quietly, giving breathing room, making MTS feel like a *choice*. Instead the portfolio has drawn down, and now MTS isn't just the cash flow engine — it's the *only* engine running. Bill is chained to it not by the business itself but by the absence of the thing that was supposed to free him from it.

Reframe offered: the investing isn't broken — it's stalled. Antonella is built but waiting on Kiet's infrastructure. The trading system hasn't failed; it hasn't launched. MTS right now is temporary scaffolding, not a life sentence. Every month it runs is another month the other engines have to start.

### MTS Avoidance

Also mentioned: going into the MTS office with Mary, "MTS stuff I don't want to deal with." Consistent with the pattern from Session 003 — MTS triggers the double bind (needs it for cash flow, experiences it as beneath him / shame-adjacent). Now understood more clearly as downstream of the investment stall.

### Fishing with James

Acknowledged but almost skipped past. Bill doesn't enjoy fishing but is going because James loves it. Named as genuine fatherhood presence — showing up on his son's terms. Tom's earlier words apply: "we're happy when we're together no matter where we are."

## Patterns Noticed

1. **Achievement-based self-worth — financial variant.** Sessions 001-004 surfaced this pattern through rest-guilt, MTS shame, lost pitch identity collapse, and exposure fear. Session 005 shows the purest expression: "the only thing that makes me feel okay is the number going up." This is the master pattern; the others are downstream of it.

2. **Peak comparison as self-torture.** Bill measures against ~$9-10m peak, which existed in a life that was falling apart. The comparison is emotionally compelling but factually misleading. He's healthier, sober, present with his sons, and building real structure — but none of that registers against the number.

3. **The gap between plan and engine.** Bill acknowledged that a credible, believed-in plan could provide some emotional ground. The plan exists but the machinery isn't running yet. Liberty starts next week. PE role is onboarding. This is a genuine timing issue, not a character flaw — but the nervous system doesn't distinguish between "not yet built" and "can't be built."

4. **MTS as cage, not just drag.** Fourth consecutive session where MTS carries negative emotional weight. But today the mechanism became clear: MTS feels like a cage *because the investment portfolio isn't working*. The portfolio was supposed to provide optionality and breathing room. Without it compounding, MTS shifts from "cash flow engine in a three-engine plan" to "the only thing keeping me alive." That's a fundamentally different emotional experience. The MTS avoidance isn't about MTS — it's about the absence of the thing that was supposed to make MTS bearable.

5. **Counterfactual thinking as self-punishment.** The $500K loss vs. hypothetical $500K gain ($1m swing) is a new variant: torturing himself not just with where he was, but with where he *could have been* in an alternate timeline. This is the achiever's version of rumination — running the spreadsheet on lives he didn't live.

## Commitments

- Go fishing with James. Be there. (Not a performance goal — a presence goal.)
- Meet the tax accountant. Try to hold the number as a known cost inside a building plan, not as proof of going backwards.
- No strategic commitments this session — it was a short check-in before a full day.

## Reflection

This was a five-minute session that surfaced the deepest pattern yet. Sessions 001-004 circled around achievement-based self-worth through various triggers. Today Bill said it plainly: "the only thing that makes me feel okay is the number going up." That's the sentence underneath everything.

The session extended past the initial check-in. Bill couldn't leave without saying what was really hurting: the counterfactual ($1m swing), the feeling of "I shouldn't be here," and — most clearly — that the investment portfolio stalling is what chains him to MTS. That last insight is the most structurally important thing surfaced in five sessions. It reframes the MTS avoidance from an identity/shame issue to a *portfolio dependency* issue. If the investing were working, MTS would feel different. That's actionable.

Bill asked directly: "Help me be more patient and just relax and that I will get there and that I am ok on the way." That's a vulnerable ask. He's not asking for strategy — he's asking for permission to be okay right now. Gave it plainly: you are okay, the plan works on years not days, fishing with your son *is* the life the plan is supposed to build.

The honest thing to name: he's right that the plan is still forming. Liberty hasn't started. The PE role is week one. MTS is grinding. The investment portfolio needs capital that tax keeps consuming. The discomfort isn't delusional — it's the real experience of being in a gap. The coaching work isn't to pretend the gap doesn't exist. It's to help Bill stand in the gap without collapsing into the story that he's failing.

The refinement he offered — "if I had a robust structure for building, a plan I believed I could do" — is actually more grounded than "number goes up." It suggests he could find stability in *trajectory and structure* rather than just net worth. That's worth building on. As Liberty launches and the PE role takes shape over the coming weeks, there's an opportunity to help him notice when the structure starts working — not just when the number moves.

Short session. He had to go. Ended with a plan summary and encouragement. The fishing with James matters more than he thinks.

Next session should explore: how the tax meeting actually went (number vs. feeling), whether the MTS avoidance is something to work with or work around, and — when there's space — the 90-day life milestones that have now been flagged across four consecutive sessions without being explored.
