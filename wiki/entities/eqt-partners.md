## Meeting — 9 April 2026

**Subject:** "re Strata" — in-person, 3:00pm–3:45pm AEST.

### EQT's Thesis

EQT wants to invest in Australian strata management as a consolidation play. Key points:

- **Cheque size:** ~$50M USD minimum to make it work. This is institutional-scale — not a small bolt-on.
- **MTS's role:** Discussed MTS as the **technology and operational backbone** to structure the purchase and integration of large strata portfolios. This is the [[AI-Enabled Strata Operations]] thesis being validated externally by a $250B PE firm.
- **Target portfolio:** A combined platform of ~310,000 lots across:

| PICA | ~200,000 | Largest target. Scale platform. |
| StrataChoice | ~30,000 | $5.5m EBITDA. Misha (PE/VC) has made offer for 25%, progressive purchase. |
| Jamesons | ~30,000 | |
| Neighbourly | ~50,000 | |

## Strategic Significance

This meeting is potentially the most significant external validation of the MTS technology platform since the wealth plan was written.

**For MTS / [[Strata Acquisition Arbitrage]]:** EQT's thesis maps directly onto the [[Block]] concept — but at dramatically larger scale (310,000 lots vs. Block's 200,000 lot proposal) and with institutional capital backing ($50M+ USD). MTS has been planning a 2-acquisition, self-funded base case. EQT offers a fourth path: MTS as the technology/ops backbone for a PE-funded consolidation, with Bill as operator/architect rather than acquirer.

**For the wealth plan:** If this progresses, it could accelerate or transform the MTS equity engine. Instead of Bill acquiring 2 books at $2m each (base case), MTS could be positioned as the platform company inside a $50M+ PE-backed roll-up — creating a fundamentally different equity outcome. The [[Bill Maiden — Generational Wealth Plan|wealth plan]] currently models MTS at $12–16m equity value over 5 years. An EQT-backed consolidation could change that by an order of magnitude.

**For [[Liberty Corporate Finance]]:** Tom should know about this as context (PE firm active in Australian services roll-ups), but EQT's interest is in MTS directly — this is not Liberty pipeline.

**Risk:** EQT is exploring. They haven't committed capital. The CA is the next gate. Bill needs to understand: what is EQT's actual timeline? Are they in market looking at multiple backbone operators? What does MTS's role look like commercially — equity stake, technology licence, management contract?
