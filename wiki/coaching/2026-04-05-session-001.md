First coaching session. Easter Sunday — a baseline reading of where Bill is at the start of the coaching relationship.

## Check-in

Bill reported feeling relaxed and hopeful. He had eaten well, was resting over Easter, and had just completed the [[Bill Maiden — Generational Wealth Plan|Generational Wealth Plan]]. He described himself as energised by learning new skills in Claude Code.

## What We Explored

**Rest and productivity entanglement.** When asked what had been happening, Bill led with relaxation and hopefulness about the plan — but then pivoted to building in Claude Code and feeling energised by new skills. The framing was revealing: *"which makes me feel I am not wasting my time."*

This was gently named as [[Achievement-Based Self-Worth]] showing up even in a moment of rest. The underlying assumption — that time without output is wasted time — was surfaced. Bill was asked: if the week had only been Easter with the boys and Agnes, with nothing "productive" to show for it, would he still feel okay about it?

**System validation.** Bill then shifted to testing whether the coaching agent was properly integrated with his knowledge base — a practical and important question. This is also consistent with the builder/achiever pattern: even during a coaching session, part of his attention goes to whether the *system* is working correctly. Not a problem — just a noticing.

## Patterns Noticed

1. **Achievement-based self-worth in rest.** Even on a holiday, Bill's sense of a "good week" includes a productivity receipt. Relaxation alone doesn't quite feel like enough. This is the core pattern described in [[Achievement-Based Self-Worth]] — worth is earned, not intrinsic.

2. **Builder energy is genuine.** The energy Bill gets from learning Claude Code is real and not purely compensatory. There is authentic enjoyment in building. The coaching task is not to suppress this but to help Bill notice when it shifts from *joy* to *justification*.

## Commitments

None explicit this session — this was a light Easter check-in and system validation.

## Reflection

This is a good baseline. Bill is in a relatively settled place — hopeful, resting, energised. The achiever pattern is present but not distressed. He is not in crisis; he is building. The risk in this phase is not collapse — it is the slow drift where building becomes the only thing that feels okay, and rest, presence, and love get squeezed out without anyone noticing.

The next session should check in on the 90-day sprint life milestones from the plan: Has he identified three things he enjoys that are not achievements? Has he had a conversation with Tom or James that wasn't about performance? Has he found someone to be honest with about how he's actually feeling?
