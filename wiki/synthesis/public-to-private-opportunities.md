## The Pattern

Antonella and Ron are identifying companies where:
1. **Share price has been crushed** — creating valuation gap vs intrinsic/PE entry price
2. **Management is strong** — CEO worth backing, not replacing
3. **PE is circling or imminent** — evidenced by strategic reviews, prior approaches, or sector dynamics
4. **Competitive landscape is weak** — competitors in trouble (e.g., Virtus underwater → MVF benefits)

Liberty's edge: approach the CEO as an ally, not an acquirer. Help management structure a deal where they participate in upside — the opposite of what PE typically offers ("punks that tell him what to do").

## Strategic Implications for Liberty

1. **Timing edge:** The trading system identifies depressed-valuation, high-short-interest, MA-converging stocks *before* PE formally approaches. This gives Liberty a window to reach CEOs first
2. **CEO-side positioning:** Most PE approaches are buy-side. Liberty positions as the CEO's advisor — helping them get the best deal rather than being the deal. This is anti-PE, which resonates (cf. Anthony Hynes' attitude)
3. **Scalable origination:** Every stock that hits the constructive base + ANTS filter is a potential P2P screen. The trading system doubles as a deal origination engine
4. **Knowledge asymmetry:** Bill's trading work means he understands these companies' technical positions (short interest, volume dynamics, valuation floor) better than a typical CF advisor calling cold
