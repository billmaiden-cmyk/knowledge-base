## Director's Read on Trading

Modra has a working machine and a paying customer base, but the FY26 forecast has been walked down four times in nine months, the FY27 order book is empty after May, and management is downsizing into the gap rather than into a plan. Cash is still healthy — but only because Interface paid early.

EBITDA forecast has dropped 42% from budget. The cash bounce in Forecast 4 is **timing only** — Interface USA paid their 2nd milestone of $2.8m early.

**Core problem:** A CreelMT sale is an 18-24 month cycle. To deliver 15-18 units a year, management needs 40-60 Tech Level 1 qualified leads in pipeline at any time. They have 16. So the FY26 shortfall is the visible part — the FY28 shortfall is already locked in by the FY26 pipeline.

**Watch list:**
- *Amber:* Tarkett relationship at risk; Milliken maintenance failed first approval; Mohawk silent for 2026; Shaw 12 deferred; Burmatex frozen; 16 of ~27 governance policies still unsigned
- *Red:* June 26 production cliff (no CMT in build); no FY27 monthly cashflow forecast in this pack; forecast credibility erosion

## Three Questions for the CEO (final set, Tuesday 28 Apr)

**Q1 — Pipeline coverage / FY28 framing:**
> "Craig, given the 18–24 month CreelMT sales cycle, the pipeline we build between now and Q3 is really shaping FY28 — not just closing the FY27 gap. Would it help the board if you walked us through a forward-rolling coverage view — qualified leads vs revenue need, by year — so we can collectively pressure-test where the highest-leverage intervention sits: adding leads, lifting conversion at a specific stage, or shortening cycle time? Happy to bring some pattern recognition from outside the carpet sector if useful."

*Why it lands:* shows the conversion math has been understood (40–60 leads → 15–18 units), reframes urgency as FY28 not FY27, offers help rather than judgment.

**Q2 — Tarkett walkaway:**
> "On Tarkett — the deferral and tariff offer reads as the right commercial posture to protect a flagship account. To give you and the team more air in that negotiation, would it be useful for the board to explicitly define our walkaway position? Specifically, the EBITDA and cash impact of the remaining 4 units converting under the new terms vs. not converting at all, and what each scenario means for the FY27 plan. That way you're not carrying the boundary call on your own."

*Why it lands:* validates the offer Craig already made, signals the new director will back hard decisions, reframes a customer risk into a governance contribution.

**Q3 — Revenue diversification ambition:**
> "The additional revenue streams work — particularly the data access, production planning software, and predictive maintenance — really catches my eye, because it's a recurring/services revenue mix that could structurally damp the CreelMT lumpiness we're seeing. What's the team's ambition for those adjacencies as a percentage of revenue at full run-rate in, say, three years? Even a directional answer would help the board decide which two or three we put real fuel behind versus which we keep in 'explore' mode."

*Why it lands:* connects the structural problem (long-cycle volatility) to the structural answer (services revenue); turns 16 ideas into a portfolio question; gives Craig room to set ambition rather than commit to numbers.

**Bonus card — Bill's textiles manufacturing background:** Yarn plant, weaving, needlefelt across AU/China/Brazil. Modra's customers (Shaw, Mohawk, Interface, Milliken) sit two steps downstream. Don't lead with it Tuesday — but deploy if discussion opens on broadloom decline (Shaw 12 cited this), end-customer behaviour, or Asian manufacturing capacity. Credibility-establishing moment.

**Held in reserve — explicit "permission for the bear case" follow-on:**
> "And on the way you work through that — would the Board benefit from a planning case at the May meeting that explicitly covers bear/base/bull on FY27 with the cost-base flex points?"

*When to deploy:* if Craig's answer to Q1 stays at the leading-indicator level without addressing the cost-base reality. Forces the bear case onto the table without putting it there yourself.

### Q1 Unpacked — How to read and respond to Craig's answer

Craig is in the worst position a CEO can be in — his own forecast process has told him the year he committed to is gone, and his Board hasn't yet given him an explicit mandate to reshape the operating model around the new reality. Until that mandate is given, every operating decision he makes is one he has to make in his own head and defend later. He cannot move at the speed the situation requires while defending against blame.

The FY28 framing in Q1 gives Craig something most CEOs in his position are starved of: a question that pulls him *forward* (build the pipeline) rather than backward (defend FY27 misses). It also makes the cycle math (40-60 leads → 15-18 units) the topic of the conversation rather than the criticism behind it.

**What this question gives Craig:**
1. **A constructive frame.** It asks where to invest, not what to cut. He can engage publicly without being defensive.
2. **A framing the Chair will support.** Coverage-by-year is exactly how Morley (BlueScope, Gerflor — both long-cycle businesses) thinks. Bill is asking the question Morley would ask if he weren't holding back to let the new director speak.
3. **A Board action item, not a CEO confession.** Positions the next decision as "what does the Board want to invest in" rather than "what has the CEO failed to do."

**What Bill is actually probing for:**
- Does Craig have a forward-rolling coverage view by year, or only a current-pipeline view? (If by-year doesn't exist, that's the real gap.)
- Where in the conversion funnel is the highest leverage — top-of-funnel volume, mid-stage conversion, or cycle-time compression? (Different leverage points imply different investments.)
- Has he tested cycle-time compression specifically — if 18-24 months is structural, what bets shorten it? (If the answer is just "the European hire", pipeline is dependent on a single hire's productivity.)
- What's his read on which customer breaks first — into an order, or out of the relationship?

**How to read Craig's answer:**
- *Structured forward-rolling view by year:* thank him, ask for a Board paper that locks the leading-indicator definition. Wind at his back.
- *Current-pipeline-only view:* gently reframe — "would it help if we asked Asraar to extend that to a year-by-year coverage view for May, so we can lock the leading indicator together?"
- *Hopeful answer ("pipeline will close"):* deploy the held-in-reserve follow-on about a planning case for May with bear/base/bull. This forces the cost-base reality without you putting it on the table.
- *No answer ready:* that itself is the answer. The May Board paper request is the right next step.

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't put a specific number on the cost base. CEO's call.
- Don't volunteer Stage 3 or specific roles.
- Don't escalate to capital.
- Don't lead with the textiles background — let it emerge if a relevant moment opens (broadloom, Asian capacity, end-customer behaviour).
