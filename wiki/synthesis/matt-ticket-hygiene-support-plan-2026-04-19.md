# Matt Wrigley — Ticket Hygiene Support Plan (VN Ops, 2026-04-19)

A proposed daily triage partner from the Vietnam operations team. The role exists to **help Matt identify what genuinely needs escalation or urgent response**, so his time is spent on the right tickets rather than on triage and status-clerking.

*Updated 2026-04-20: Role reframed as a triage partner for Matt. Daily reporting to Bill removed; weekly metrics dashboard removed. The VN ops person works for Matt, not above him.*

## Objective

Matt starts each billable block with a clear view of what actually needs him, and a second set of eyes that surfaces items that should not sit longer — without adding an administrative burden or a reporting overlay.

## Proposed structure — 1 hour per day, 2×30-minute passes

### AM pass — ~9:00–9:30 AEST *(start of Matt's day)*

**Purpose:** Identify the genuine escalations from overnight and set priority order for the day.

**Tasks:**
1. Scan all tickets updated since previous pass.
2. **Flag escalations to Matt** — compliance risks (fire orders, safety), items approaching or past SLA breach, clients who've re-engaged after a long silence, anything smelling urgent. One short Slack DM to Matt listing what genuinely needs him first.
3. Pin new requester replies at the top of Matt's "respond first" view.
4. Hygiene pass — reclassify Open → Pending where Matt was last to update and the requester hasn't responded. Leave a note in the ticket.
5. Flag probable duplicates (same building, same subject thread) — propose merge to Matt.
6. File resolved tickets to Box per retention policy.

### Midday pass — ~12:30–13:00 AEST *(after Matt's morning billable work)*

**Purpose:** Pick up what's changed, surface drifting items, prep the afternoon.

**Tasks:**
1. Move any ticket Matt responded to this morning from Open → Pending if the reply closes the loop.
2. Confirm afternoon priorities with Matt — which items from the AM list got touched, which still need him?
3. Scan stale-leaning tickets (>21 days since any activity) — draft a one-line chase note or close/merge recommendation for Matt's decision.
4. Draft first-reply templates for recurring request types (fire inspection notices, levy queries, routine defects intake) — Matt reviews and signs off weekly.

## Reporting — Matt only

- **To Matt:** short Slack DMs flagging escalations and afternoon priorities. Conversational, not formatted. No scoreboards.
- **To Bill:** none on a daily or weekly basis. This is support for Matt, not surveillance. If Matt wants to discuss how the support is working, it surfaces in the 1:1 conversationally.
- **To Kat:** ops-level oversight as line manager of the VN ops person — exception-based only (e.g. scope creep, resourcing issues).

## What the VN ops person does NOT do

- **Does not respond substantively** to client questions on defects, fire compliance, remedial works, or any regulated matter. Matt is the expert and licensed builder.
- **Does not close tickets without Matt's say-so** on anything other than clear admin duplicates.
- **Does not change scope or commitments** on behalf of Matt or MTS.
- **Does not contact committees, suppliers, or consultants directly** unless explicitly delegated ticket by ticket.
- **Does not report ticket metrics to Bill.**

Their remit is: escalation guidance, status, sequencing, filing, and drafting templates. Not content. Not reporting.

## Who

A single nominated VN ops person for continuity — same person each day builds familiarity with Matt's portfolio and his regular contacts. Criteria to discuss with Kat and Quang:

- Someone from the Vietnam team with admin/ops strength (not a developer)
- English comfort to read Zendesk threads and draft short Slack updates
- 1 hour/day commitment, Monday–Friday
- Backup nominated for leave coverage

## Matt's side of the structure

For the support to work, Matt needs to:

1. Check the AM escalation DM within the first 15 minutes of his day.
2. Act on genuine escalations before other billable work, or tell the VN ops person why not.
3. Maintain the pinned "respond-first" view — reorder it if his judgment differs.
4. Review and sign off first-reply templates weekly (one Friday slot, ~10 min).

## Trial & review

- **Trial length:** 4 weeks, week of 20 April to week of 18 May 2026.
- **Weekly check:** 15-minute Friday call between Matt and the VN ops person — what counted as an escalation, what was missed, template refinements.
- **End-of-trial review (end May):** Matt and Bill discuss whether the support is helping. Decision to continue, adjust, or stand down is a conversation, not a metrics threshold.
- **Escalation path:** Matt or VN ops person flags issues to Kat (Operations Manager) in the first instance.

## Cost / benefit framing

- **Cost:** ~5 hrs/week of VN ops time. Marginal.
- **Benefit:** Matt's billable time is protected from triage overhead, and genuinely urgent items do not slip. The support is judged by whether Matt finds it useful — not by dashboards.
