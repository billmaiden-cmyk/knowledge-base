More Than Strata (MTS) is a Sydney-based strata management business founded and 100%-owned by [[Bill Maiden]]. It is the primary owned wealth engine in his generational wealth plan, with a current market value of approximately $2.5m and a projected equity value of ~$20m by Year 5 of the plan.

## Historical Baseline (February 2023)

The February 2023 board presentation captures MTS's state approximately 3 years before the current plans were articulated:

| Metric | Feb 2023 Value |
|--------|---------------|
| Lots managed | ~889 (5 weeks ahead of target) |
| Full-year lot target (FY23) | 1,128 lots |
| Monthly revenue (Jan 2023) | ~$40.7K |
| Monthly OPEX (Jan 2023) | ~$57.1K |
| Net profit (Jan 2023) | **($16.2K)** — running at a loss |
| Cash balance | ~$73–76K |
| Pipeline (estimated conversion) | 108 lots confirmed + 255 lots future |

This baseline is notable because it shows MTS was unprofitable and sub-scale in early 2023 — a $40K/month revenue business with $57K OPEX. The progression from this baseline to the ~$1.4M annual revenue and 2,000-lot position in the 2025–26 plan reflects substantial organic growth achieved in the intervening two years.

**Top 10 clients by annual management fee (Feb 2023):** Brick Lane (~$28.5K), Phar Lap Gardens (~$27.3K), Arcadia (~$23.8K), Huntleys Cove (~$22.8K), Motif (~$19.8K), Endeavour (~$17.7K), Waitara (~$14K), Golden Sands (~$13.8K), Hurlstone Park (~$12.2K), Yagoona (~$11.2K).

## Operations

MTS runs client service through Zendesk. Day-to-day issues include repairs and maintenance, special levies, access disputes, and committee politics. Staff include Matt and Mary (both sources of friction for Bill) and Kat (who owns operations as the plan progresses).

Bill experiences MTS emotionally as an obligation rather than a calling — "beneath his capabilities." He describes it as a "$1.3m strata firm" when he believes he should be running a $100m platform.

## AI Swarm and Automation

See [[AI-Enabled Strata Operations]].

Bill has hired two AI engineers in Ho Chi Minh City (Vietnam) building:
- An AI agent swarm around Zendesk: R&M Concierge, inbox triage, meeting pack builder, AI COO/CFO/CCO/CTO roles

The aim: materially lower cost-to-serve, increase lots per FTE, improve service quality, and make MTS/Block a scalable AI-advantaged platform.

## Growth Model

MTS operates on a step-function cost model. AI enables each capacity step to absorb 2,200 organic lots before a new resource is needed ($300k/step). Within each band, organic growth adds revenue at near-zero incremental cost. At full utilization, gross profit reaches the 70% standard.

**Organic growth targets:**
- Small buildings: ramp from 2.5 to 5.0 per month over months 1–6 (12 lots each, $600/lot/year)
- Large buildings: 1 every 3 months (70 lots each, $400/lot/year)

**Acquisition strategy (2 base case):** See [[Strata Acquisition Arbitrage]].
- Each acquisition: ~$1.1m revenue, $2.0m purchase price (~3× management fees), $300k incremental COGS
- Acquisition 1: Month 13 | Acquisition 2: Month 25
- Fully debt-funded; LTV never exceeds ~32%

## General Manager (Hard Condition)

A GM must be hired by Month 13 (Year 2) at $250k/year. Without a GM, the plan is not emotionally sustainable — MTS becomes a daily operational burden for Bill. From Month 13, Bill's MTS involvement drops to ~3–6 hours/week (Monday morning + Friday morning check-in).

If a capable GM cannot be found by Month 15, the signal is to sell MTS.

## Block Merger Proposal

Michael Teys ([[Block]]) wants to merge MTS with Strata Embassy, add a further 2,000+ lots, and build an AI-powered roll-up vehicle targeting a $500m EV. Bill sees upside but also loss of control and risk of re-entrapment.

## Key Risks

- AI margin proof fails — if AI doesn't deliver 70% GP and 2,200-lot capacity steps, the acquisition arbitrage disappears and MTS remains a $2.5m service book
- GM not hired — Bill remains daily operator and plan becomes psychologically unsustainable
- Bill re-absorbs MTS operations in a moment of stress (his identified behavioral pattern)
