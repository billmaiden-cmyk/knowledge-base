Bill Maiden's public brand strategy is a 3–5 year plan to become Australia's leading AI executive voice, using [[More Than Strata (MTS)]] as the launch pad and targeting $500M–$1B in personal equity through a combination of business exits, a PE fund, and consulting/advisory revenues.

> **Document timeline:** Both documents are ~2024 ChatGPT-assisted strategy outputs representing the early articulation of Bill's personal positioning thesis. They were produced before the more refined pitch deck work in January 2026 (see [[AI Leadership Consulting Platform]]). The Monetisation doc sets out the larger ambition (6 pillars, $500M–$1B target, PE fund); the AI-Driven Value Creation doc is the more actionable near-term plan (3-year, Year 1 specific targets). Together they represent the foundational strategic framing — the "why and what" before the "how" was worked up in detail.

## The Core Objective

Position Bill as Australia's most credible and visible AI executive, with a parallel commercial strategy that monetises that position across multiple vehicles: direct MTS value creation, an AI-focused PE fund, speaking and advisory fees, and ultimately a global platform exit.

Target: **$500M–$1B personal equity within 3–5 years.**

## Six Strategic Pillars (Monetisation & Positioning doc)

| Pillar | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **1. National AI Profile** | Establish credibility as Australia's leading AI executive voice via content, media, and industry leadership |
| **2. AI Platform Company** | MTS as the proof-of-concept: demonstrating AI-enabled transformation of a traditional service business |
| **3. PE Fund — AI Transformation Holdings** | Launch an AI-focused PE fund; initial target raise: $50–100M; deploy into businesses where AI can create step-change value |
| **4. Showcase Success** | Use MTS wins (margin expansion, automation) as visible case studies that amplify the brand narrative |
| **5. MTS Launch Pad** | MTS is not the destination — it is the proof platform and the credibility anchor for everything else |
| **6. Global Expansion** | Take the proven Australian model global; strata/body corporate markets in comparable jurisdictions |

## Credibility Assessment

The AI-Driven Value Creation doc is candid about the current credibility gap:

- Bill is positioned as a **"pragmatic implementer"** — a business operator applying AI to a real problem, not a technologist or researcher
- This is a strength in the operations and investing world, but not in deep-tech circles
- The strategy leans into the implementer identity: demonstrate real business outcomes (margin, speed, cost) rather than claiming technical depth

## Year 1 Priorities and Targets (AI-Driven Value Creation doc)

### MTS AI Integration Milestones (Year 1)
| Target | Metric |
|--------|--------|
| Response speed | 50% faster via AI triage/automation |
| Query automation | 30–40% of inbound queries resolved without human intervention |
| Productivity | 20% productivity boost per manager |
| Revenue growth | 50–100% revenue growth (organic + AI-enabled new capacity) |

### Brand-Building Actions (Year 1)
- Document and share the MTS transformation in public channels (LinkedIn, industry events)
- Develop initial speaking profile at PropTech and AI in business forums
- **Lost-proposal campaign**: Contact 250 past MTS prospects; 10% conversion target → ~25 new strata schemes

### Credibility Levers
- Published case studies from MTS transformation
- Quantified outcomes (cost saved, margin achieved, time automated)
- Speaking at PropTech Australia and national strata industry events

## Year 2: Scaling and Validation

- MTS target valuation: $7–10M (vs ~$2.5M starting base)
- Begin formalising the consulting/advisory offering, supported by MTS case study material
- Explore initial investor conversations for AI Transformation Holdings PE fund
- Acquisitions starting (see [[Strata Acquisition Arbitrage]]): 2 debt-funded acquisitions prove the roll-up model works

## Year 3: Three Exit Paths

| Path | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Scale and exit MTS** | Sell MTS at platform multiple ($20–40M+ at 6–8× EBITDA) after proving AI margin thesis |
| **Launch venture/fund** | Use MTS success as anchor case for AI Transformation Holdings; raise $50–100M |
| **Corporate leadership** | Step into a C-suite or board AI leadership role at a major Australian company, using brand as the credential |

These are presented as parallel options, not a single forced path. Bill retains optionality by building credentials that are valuable across all three.

## PE Fund: AI Transformation Holdings

A proposed AI-focused private equity vehicle:
- **Thesis**: identify traditional service businesses where AI can compress cost structure by 30–50% and re-rate valuation multiples from labour-service to tech-platform levels
- **Initial raise target**: $50–100M
- **Deployment strategy**: 3–5 portfolio companies; apply MTS playbook as the template
- **Bill's role**: Fund Manager / CIO, drawing on MTS as proof-of-concept

This extends the [[Strata Acquisition Arbitrage]] logic beyond strata — the core insight (buy at labour margins, sell at AI-platform margins) is sector-agnostic.

## MTS as Launch Pad

The strategy is explicit: MTS is not the end state. It is the proving ground that:
1. Validates the AI margin thesis with real P&L
2. Creates documented case study material for brand-building
3. Generates the cash flows and equity to fund personal wealth accumulation
4. Provides the credibility anchor for consulting, PE fund, and speaking opportunities

The risk: if MTS fails to deliver the AI margin proof point within 6–12 months, the entire brand narrative loses its foundation. See [[AI-Enabled Strata Operations]].

## Relationship to the Generational Wealth Plan

The brand strategy sits alongside the financial plan (see [[Bill Maiden — Generational Wealth Plan]]). They are complementary:
- The financial plan optimises the asset base (MTS equity, investments, Liberty CF)
- The brand strategy maximises the value of the human capital asset (Bill's expertise and profile)
- At Year 3–5, they converge: a high-profile exit + fund launch + advisory revenues creates the $500M–$1B outcome
