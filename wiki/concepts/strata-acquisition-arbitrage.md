Strata acquisition arbitrage is the value creation mechanism at the core of [[More Than Strata (MTS)]]'s growth plan.

> **Document timeline:** The institutional roll-up business plan (~2024) sets out the larger-scale five-company model. The personal wealth plan (~2025) is a more conservative synthesis that adopts a two-acquisition base case as the "honest" plan Bill wants to build his life around. The ~2025 thinking scales *down* from the ~2024 ambition — not because the larger model is wrong, but because it carries control and commitment costs that conflict with Bill's personal priorities. Both documents remain valid; they answer different questions. It exploits the spread between the price at which strata management books are acquired (based on traditional industry margins) and the price at which the consolidated, AI-enabled platform is eventually valued (based on significantly higher margins).

## The Arbitrage

| | Entry (acquisition) | Exit (platform valuation) |
|---|---|---|
| EBITDA margin assumed | ~20% | 40–53% |
| Valuation multiple | ~2.5× acquired EBITDA | ~6–6.5× platform EBITDA |
| Implied spread | — | ~2.5× to 2.6× |

In concrete terms for the MTS base case:
- Each acquisition costs ~$2.0m (~3× management fees, or ~2.5× the EBITDA the book generates *on Bill's platform*)
- Each acquisition adds ~$800k/year in incremental gross profit (at 73% incremental margin, enabled by [[AI-Enabled Strata Operations]])
- The consolidated platform is valued at 6–6.5× EBITDA at exit
- The spread between 2.5× entry and 6–6.5× exit creates substantial per-acquisition equity value

## MTS Base Case: Two Acquisitions

| Assumption | Value |
|---|---|
| Acquisitions | 2 (Month 13 and Month 25) |
| Revenue per acquisition | $1.1m/year |
| Purchase price | $2.0m each |
| Incremental COGS | $300k/year |
| Incremental GP | $800k/year (73% margin) |
| Funding | Fully debt-funded |

**Debt facility terms:**
- Valuation method: 3× annualized management fees
- LTV limit: 70% of group market value (never exceeded ~32% in the model)
- Interest rate: 8% p.a., 5-year fully amortizing
- Interest coverage covenant: 2.0× minimum (never remotely tested; actual coverage ~10×+)

Because acquisitions are fully debt-funded, Bill puts in zero additional equity. The borrowing base expands with each acquisition's management fees, enabling the next acquisition to be funded by the growing group valuation.

## Why Two Acquisitions

Two is described as the "honest base case":
- One is too conservative for the wealth path Bill wants
- Four is a stretch case, not a plan to build a life around

Even without any acquisitions, organic growth alone would produce approximately $8–10m in MTS equity at Year 5 at 5–6× — so the acquisitions are additive to a base that works independently.

## Sensitivity

If integration costs are $400–500k instead of $300k:
- Each acquisition still generates $700k incremental EBITDA
- The arbitrage weakens but remains strongly positive

If suitable 2,000-lot books are unavailable in Sydney at ~$2m:
- The organic-only plan still generates meaningful equity
- The Block roll-up path (see [[Block]]) is an alternative route to scale

## Relationship to AI Margins

The arbitrage only works because [[AI-Enabled Strata Operations]] allows acquired books to be absorbed onto the existing cost infrastructure with $300k incremental cost per 2,200-lot step. If AI margins fail, integration costs rise to traditional staffing levels and the spread disappears.

## Roll-Up Business Plan: Five-Company Model

A more detailed business plan (source: Business Plan_ Leveraging MTS AI.pdf) sets out the institutional-scale version of this arbitrage — acquiring approximately five strata management companies and deploying the MTS AI platform across the combined portfolio.

### Financial Structure

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Target EBITDA (at acquisition) | $10M combined (5 companies) |
| Entry multiple | ~4× EBITDA |
| Acquisition cost | ~$40M |
| Debt funding (Macquarie Bank) | ~70% ($28M) |
| Equity required | ~$12M |
| Target EBITDA post-AI (Year 2–3) | $30M (3× increase) |
| Exit multiple | ~8× EBITDA |
| Exit enterprise value | ~$240M |
| Return on equity (MOIC) | ~6× |
| Executive carry pool (20%) | ~$40M |

The debt structure uses Macquarie Bank as the preferred lender, consistent with their established relationship in the strata sector.

### Why 4× Entry / 8× Exit

The entry multiple of 4× reflects current industry pricing for traditional strata managers (labour-intensive, 15–20% EBITDA margins). The exit at 8× reflects a fundamental re-rating: the combined platform is no longer a labour service business but an AI-enabled tech platform with 40–53% EBITDA margins and compounding operational intelligence. Acquirers at exit are paying for the margin profile, the automation infrastructure, and the scalable template — not just the lot count.

### Five-Phase Execution Roadmap

| Phase | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1. Targeting | Identify and prioritise 5 acquisition targets (criteria: size, margin profile, geography, seller motivation) |
| 2. DD / Close | Due diligence and transaction execution for each target |
| 3. Integration | Migrate acquired books onto MTS systems (Zendesk, StrataMax, Box) |
| 4. AI Enhancement | Deploy AI swarm workflows across integrated portfolio; drive margin expansion |
| 5. Exit Preparation | Consolidate reporting, normalise EBITDA, prepare vendor DD materials |

### Management Team Economics

The 20% carry (~$40M on a $240M exit) is structured to attract and retain the management team executing the roll-up. This is treated as a distinct incentive pool from Bill's founder equity.
