This synthesis covers Bill Maiden's complete generational wealth strategy as documented in the April 2026 wealth plan. It integrates the financial architecture, psychological constraints, operating rhythm, and family legacy objectives into a coherent whole.

## The One-Sentence Plan

> Invest [[Bill Maiden]]'s time in [[Liberty Corporate Finance]] where his hour is most valuable, run [[More Than Strata (MTS)]] as a GM-led AI-enabled platform that creates $20m in equity through operating leverage and 2 debt-funded acquisitions, compound all after-tax surplus cash at 18% in a disciplined portfolio, and reach $53m in 5 years and $83m in 10 years — while living a life that includes being present for Tom, James, and Agnes, and not being consumed by the strata grind.

## Why This Plan, For This Person

The plan is designed not just around numbers but around [[Bill Maiden]]'s specific psychological profile. See [[Achievement-Based Self-Worth]].

Bill evaluates every opportunity through four lenses: safety vs threat, status vs humiliation, autonomy vs entrapment, and whether he is setting himself up to carry a giant burden alone. Some wealth paths that work on a spreadsheet would be emotionally unsustainable for him. The plan explicitly states:

> *"The plan must be ambitious but must not depend on Bill being the daily operational grinder and emotional shock absorber for a services business."*

## Starting Position (April 2026)

Net worth ~$5.0m:
- MTS: $2.5m (100% owned)
- Investment portfolio: $2.0m (100% owned)
- Manuflo/other: $0.5m
- Liberty Corporate Finance: ~$100k cost base (16% equity + 12% profit share)

## The Three-Engine Architecture

See [[Compounding Wealth Architecture]] for the full model.

**Engine 1 — MTS:** 100%-owned equity compounding through AI margins, organic growth, 2 acquisitions. GM-led from Month 13. $20.4m equity + $3.1m cash at Year 5.

**Engine 2 — Liberty:** Bill's PE relationships and judgment converting directly into fee income and 16% equity appreciation. $18.5m total value + $3.4m cumulative cash at Year 5.

**Engine 3 — Investment Portfolio:** All surplus cash from Engines 1 and 2 compounding at 18% net. $14.2m at Year 5, potentially $45–50m at Year 10 if MTS sale proceeds are added.

## Critical Dependencies (What Must Be True)

1. **AI margins prove real by Month 6** — The single most critical dependency. If 70% GP and 2,200-lot/step capacity don't materialize, sell MTS at $2.5m and pivot.
2. **GM in place by Month 13** — A hard condition, not a preference. Without it, the plan is psychologically unsustainable.
3. **Bill stays out of MTS daily operations** — His time must go to Liberty (Tue–Thu) not MTS (Mon morning + Fri morning only).
4. **Portfolio discipline** — Written investment policy with hard position limits, correlation controls, and drawdown triggers.
5. **Liberty scales** — Requires Bill's strategic contribution, not just Tom's execution.

## Family Legacy

A family holding entity (trust or company, Australian tax-optimized) will hold MTS equity/proceeds, Liberty equity/proceeds, and portions of the investment portfolio.

Tom (14) and James (12) are being trained as stewards:
- **Now:** Real investment accounts ($5–10k each), real decisions, monthly review with Bill. Real tasks in MTS and research.
- **By 18:** 4–6 years of investment experience, operational exposure, AI fluency, understanding of the holding structure.
- **By 22–25:** Ability to propose ventures to the holding company for seed funding; operational roles available; portions of the portfolio to manage.

The principle: capital access is earned through demonstrated capability. The inheritance is a platform — capital, businesses, technology, relationships, and skills — not passive wealth.

## Sensitivity / Downside

Even in the stressed scenario (AI fails, Liberty at 50% capacity, portfolio at 12%): Year 5 net worth is ~$18–22m. Still a meaningful outcome for a person starting at $5m.

At base, even the low-end valuation scenario (MTS at 5× rather than 6.5×) produces a Year 5 total of ~$48m.

## Psychological Design Principles Embedded in the Plan

1. Bill does not handle client complaints or staff issues — the GM does
2. MTS is capped at 5 hours/week — structural, not aspirational
3. Liberty is the primary work identity from Month 6 onward
4. The investment portfolio has written rules, not emotional discretion
5. Personal milestones (sleep, presence with sons, enjoyable activities) are tracked alongside business milestones
6. The hardest part is not strategy — it is restraint
