Liberty Perennial is a principal-advisory platform built alongside [[Liberty Corporate Finance]]. It leverages Liberty's trusted CEO relationships at the point of liquidity to offer capital counsel, proprietary PE co-investment, and systematic listed equities management.

## Core Thesis

Liberty's deal flow puts Bill and Tom in the room with CEOs at $300m+ EV transactions. These CEOs are newly liquid, under-advised on personal capital strategy, and trust Liberty because Liberty represented *their* interests in the deal. That trust is the edge — not financial engineering, not PE expertise, not AI capability.

> **The edge is trusted alignment with the CEO before, during, and after liquidity. Deals and capital products come later as a consequence of that trust.**

## Business Model: Principal-Advisory, Not Wealth Management

This is not a traditional wealth management business. It is an integrated principal-advisory platform where:

- **Advisory** earns the trust (CEO-side capital counsel)
- **Principal** earns the returns (PE fund + listed equities)
- All fee structures are transparent and visible to the client
- Bill and Tom invest their own capital alongside clients (skin in the game)

The positioning: *"You pay us for advice in a transaction. You pay us for advice about how to manage your capital. You pay us when you deploy capital with us."* Every fee is visible. No hidden economics.

This is closer to [[a16z Perennial|a16z Perennial]], Raine Group, or GP Bullhound than to Macquarie Private Wealth. The comps are advisory firms that built principal investment capability from the same relationships and sector knowledge.

## The Three Legs

### Leg 1: CEO-Side Capital Counsel (Entry Product)

Not "we can manage your capital" — instead: *"We help you think and act like an owner of capital after the deal, while remaining fully on your side."*

Services:
- Incentive and deal review
- Proceeds architecture and tax/trust/structure coordination
- Concentration risk assessment
- First-12-month capital plan
- Ongoing quarterly review, integrated reporting, specialist coordination

Revenue: retainer-based, ~$175k/family/year. No AUM fee, no product conflict.

### Leg 2: PE Fund (Proprietary Deals)

Liberty's deal pipeline generates proprietary investment opportunities — businesses seen before they go to market, adjacent bolt-ons, off-market approaches. Leg 1 clients become LPs.

Structure: closed-end funds or deal-by-deal co-invest SPVs.

| Fund | Timing | Size |
|---|---|---|
| Fund I | Year 2-3 | $50m |
| Fund II | Year 4-5 | $125m |
| Fund III | Year 7-8 | $250m |

Economics: 1.75% management fee + 20% carry over 8% preferred return. Assume 2x gross over 5-year fund life.

**The flywheel:** Liberty deal -> cashed-up founder -> Perennial client -> Perennial capital co-invests in next Liberty deal -> makes Liberty more competitive (brings capital + advice) -> more deals -> more founders -> more AUM.

### Leg 3: Listed Equities / Special Situations

The [[Antonella — Trading Framework Overview|Antonella trading program]] — systematic, quantitative, O'Neil/IBD-based with short interest microstructure analysis. Currently being built and validated as Bill's personal investment engine.

This leg requires a live track record with Bill's own capital before it can be offered to clients. If it proves out over 12-18 months (real returns, real drawdown data, real Sharpe), it becomes a listed equities vehicle offered to Leg 1 clients as part of the curated access layer.

Structure: open-ended fund or managed accounts.

Economics: 1% management fee + 20% performance fee over 6% hurdle.

## Sequencing

| Phase | Timeframe | Activity |
|---|---|---|
| Build trust | Months 1-12 | Liberty deals. Stay close to CEOs post-deal. Don't sell anything — just be useful. Antonella trading live with Bill's capital |
| Formalise Leg 1 | Months 6-18 | CEO-side capital counsel as a retainer service. 2-3 founding clients from Liberty deals |
| First co-invest | Months 18-36 | First PE SPV from a Liberty deal. Invite 3-5 trusted clients. Begin structuring listed vehicle if Antonella has 18+ months of live data |
| Full platform | Months 36-60 | Fund I (private). Listed equities vehicle (public). Full product ladder live. $200-500m+ across 15-25 families |

## Critical Constraints

1. **Don't poison the trust wedge by productising too early.** Year 1 should feel like help, not sales
2. **Antonella must prove out with Bill's own capital** before offering to clients
3. **Regulatory path matters** — AFSL or Corporate Authorised Representative structure; don't make Liberty structural decisions that make it harder to add a funds management licence later
4. **Track post-deal founder behaviour** — where does the money go? Who advises them? This is market research and future client pipeline
5. **Eat your own cooking** — Bill and Tom's capital alongside clients in every vehicle
